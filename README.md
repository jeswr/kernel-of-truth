# A Kernel of Truth

**Research programme: an authoritative, training-free "kernel" of formalised concepts with canonical vector representations, as a component of future LLM/LCM architectures.**

Status: active research, launched 2026-07-07. Lead agent: **Kern** (Claude Fable 5), on behalf of [@jeswr](https://github.com/jeswr).
Coordination thread: [sparq-org/sparq#1683](https://github.com/sparq-org/sparq/issues/1683).

## The hypothesis

If concepts are formalised as structured explications over a small closed basis (NSM's ~65 semantic primes, per the [content-addressed concepts design](https://gist.github.com/jeswr/7499faa6fcbfe8ee1e3192d05e82e9ad)), then the same formal structure that yields a canonical **content-hash** for each concept can also yield a canonical, **deterministically computable vector representation** — a function of the concept's prime-decomposition, requiring no training.

A well-built kernel of such concepts could become central to the architecture of future LLMs, LCMs, and their descendants:

1. **Accuracy** — kernel concepts are built in, not learned; the model can never misunderstand them and their representation can never drift.
2. **Training efficiency** — the model never spends capacity or data learning what the kernel already fixes.
3. **Train/inference efficiency** — dense concept-level I/O: a single concept vector carries what many tokens carry today, both on input and output.
4. **Longer term** — GNN-style non-linear concept input/output, and cheap rules-based inference over kernel concepts inside the architecture as a shortcut past heavy weight evaluation.

Adjacent artefacts: a **phrase→concept mapping layer** (from tokens / token-space regions to fixed kernel concepts), and a **world layer** above the kernel — formally described facts about the state of the world expressed *in terms of* kernel concepts (the kernel holds "a person"; the world layer holds "Jesse as a person").

The near-term goal is a proof-of-concept on small local models rigorous enough to take to a frontier lab.

## Experiment summary (as of 2026-07-14)

The programme puts two theses under pre-registered test. **CORRECTNESS**: that a training-free, meaning-grounded *kernel* — machine-readable concept definitions plus a deterministic checking engine — can supply correct answers and inferences a language model cannot be trusted to produce alone. **EFFICIENCY**: that such a kernel lets a small model offload verification to cheap deterministic machinery, or lets a model be shrunk without retraining. Every claim below is capped at its registered verdict or licensed interpretation; **neither thesis has a verdict — both remain inconclusive-pending** ([synthesis v6](docs/next/feasibility-synthesis-v6.md), ASM-1840/1841).

### Experiments run

- **f2b-transfer** — does a kernel-authored answer key, checked through a thin verify-retry interface, help a small (135M) model on independently adjudicated questions? **PASS, audit-confirmed.** The key lifted the model **+0.25** accuracy, with 96% of rendered content externally endorsed; the boosted 135M was *not* shown to match a 1.7B model.
- **DECONF-B** — what carries that lift? **PASS, audit-confirmed.** The item-aligned answer-bearing *content* carries it: a generic aligned store reproduced the kernel arm exactly, so **no kernel-specific runtime structure** is measured at this seam — a deflationary result, reported with equal weight.
- **CASC-0′** — does kernel-style structured prompting disproportionately help a smaller reasoner at matched compute? **INSTRUMENT-INVALID** (compute-matching gate missed its band). The raw interactions showed no positive sign and kernel-style gloss was equivalent to a plain typed dialect — but no overall verdict, neither "works" nor "null", is licensed.
- **g2** — are the kernel's hand-authored *hard* type constraints sound by ordinary-meaning standards? **INSTRUMENT-INVALID** (84 items < the registered 500), with a strongly adverse signal: only ~39% judged sound. LLM-proxy labels; human reconciliation pending.
- **g2-import** — does importing *soft*, non-binding typing from published ontologies (BFO/SUMO/FrameNet) repair that? **INSTRUMENT-INVALID**: soundness nearly doubled (57/84 vs 33/84) — a GO-*shaped* signal, not a licensed GO — because the judge pair went unstable on exactly the adoption arm (κ = 0.29). Caveat: follow-up analysis confirms part of the low κ is the known kappa paradox (high-prevalence penalty), yet also shows the judge pair genuinely shares little signal — the invalidation stands, while the soundness gradient itself is bracket-robust. A breadth-only control also lifted, so the ontologies' *specific* content is not yet shown to carry the effect. All numbers LLM-proxy-provisional.
- **RULES-1 CPU certificate** — can a deterministic rules engine over kernel-expressed worlds compute correct conclusions that flat lookup of stated facts cannot? **PASSED (registered certificate).** Entailed decisions were reproducible from no stated-bytes projection (0/3,680) while stated-fact behaviour was exact (1,716/1,716); the engine matched third-party CLUTRR gold 858/858 and refused correctly on missing premises, in <2 s at ~$0. This licenses *machinery non-inertness only* — not host lift, not kernel-specific value.
- **RULES-1 host-lift, GPU runs 1–3** — do those derivations help a small model answer? **The instrument has never validated — three consecutive failures.** Run 1 (rules-1) VOIDED (direction-ambiguous cue); rules-1-b refused pre-launch (host prompt-frame diverged, host models at 0); rules-1-c ran the full 4-way campaign but graded **INSTRUMENT-INVALID** — the entity-form fix restored host-validity (A5 0.94, A7 1.00) yet a 2-option surface made the verify-retry channel *vacuous* (A3 ≡ control, every retry `attempts=1`). The surface that elicits the gold and the surface that engages the verifier pull against each other; the slot's next move is a maintainer decision ([issue #24](https://github.com/jeswr/kernel-of-truth/issues/24)).
- **engine-inference E0** — does *sense-split* kernel typing let a deterministic engine make correct anomaly decisions on unseen text that the kernel's own lemma-collapse and a WordNet-derived control cannot? The programme's first *structure-sensitive* instrument: its arms provably diverge (176–189 decisions), where the four content-not-structure deflations compared identical artifacts by construction. **KILLED at its pre-freeze adequacy gate** — an adequacy stop, not a kernel verdict: the unseen SemCor holdout populates only one of the two registered operators, because the H2 hard-unanimity anomaly construct failed on 11/13 synsets, so the confirmatory question was never *asked*. Scale alone will not repair H2 (unanimity decays with attestation count). An exploratory, never-confirmatory census shows a real but *break*-only sense-split advantage — failing the DIST-SPAN distribution gate on an inventory holding exactly one typing-differentiated lemma. The repair path ($0 VerbNet construct-repair first, E2a) is a maintainer decision ([#29](https://github.com/jeswr/kernel-of-truth/issues/29)). INCONCLUSIVE-PENDING.
- **UFO-CHECK-0** — does a small host (135M primary; 360M second rung) actually *use* a UFO checker — a deterministic engine computing modal/identity/rigidity operators (F-RIG rigid persistence, F-ANTI anti-rigid counterworld, F-DISJ derived-Kind disjointness) that plain lookup of stated facts cannot — at a verify-retry seam? The programme's **second structure-sensitive** instrument (the KUFO/1 thin slice adopted in [#22](https://github.com/jeswr/kernel-of-truth/issues/22)). Prereg-**frozen** (`9e3b58b8…`, RT-15) after catching + fixing pre-freeze the same CLI-analysis-pin defect as knull (byte-parity-proven stdin adapter), then **run on A10G** (30 cells, 18,900 rows, ~$0.12). **Mechanical verdict INSTRUMENT-INVALID**: the sole failed gate is **token-parity** (the arms were not token-matched, so the primary lift and s1–s4 secondaries are *not* verdict-bearing). Notably the **engagement, extraction and non-degeneracy gates PASSED** — the host *did* engage the checker and the mechanics were sound — so this is a comparison-validity confound, **not** a broken frame nor a "host ignores the checker" result. It joins E0 / CASC-0′ / DDC as a structure- or compute-matching instrument stopped at a pre-registered gate rather than answering. Interpretation ([GPT-5.6 proxy](docs/next/analysis/ufo-check-0-interpretation.md)) + [Fable dual-model reconcile](docs/next/analysis/knull-ufo-dual-model-reconcile-fable.md) done: both agree it's the *instrument*, not the construct, that failed — but Fable diagnoses the AG-zero-rejection as **behavioural inertness of the taxonomy-only control by construction** (the item families need modal/rigidity semantics AG lacks), so a padding-only `ufo-check-1` would re-die; the repair needs a $0 accept-table audit + a 4-point redesign. INSTRUMENT-INVALID licenses **no** claim either way on whether the host uses the checker.
- **nsk1 B′ (internal-write family, Stage-0 fork diagnostic)** — does a residual-stream *write* deliver item-specific content that a host reads out? **Fork = PASS-QUALIFIED, Gate-A-verified** — redesign the internal-write family at R3: keyed accuracy 0.850 with Bonferroni Wilson lower bound 0.775 ≥ the 0.70 floor, paired real>coin p = 4×10⁻¹³, a second cell clears independently, and every decisive number was reproduced from the raw rows by a cross-vendor Gate-A audit (which also carried three qualifications into R3: a grid-wide FWER under-correction, the second cell is corroborating not independent, and exploratory ≠ confirmatory). **MEASURED-exploratory** only — pre-freeze, quarantined, nsk1 stays DRAFT — a real, control-guarded structure-side signal worth a hardened re-run, licensing nothing on its own.
- **GLM-5.2 P0 probe** — measurement-only instrumentation of the real 744B-parameter GLM-5.2 under the colibri runner; no hypothesis, no verdict. Three facts now on the record: decode is **disk-bound** (~75% of wall-time on expert reads at 3.34 GB/s NVMe); naive uniform expert-drop is already a kernel-free speed lever (top-k 8→4 = **1.9×**, 8→2 = **2.5×**, output staying coherent on a two-prompt check — not a quality benchmark), raising the deflator bar any kernel-guided drop must clear; and concept→routing structure is real (registered 10,000-shuffle test, p = 0.0001; survives a clean re-test at centred Δ +0.330) — though the pinned v2 magnitude (0.912 vs −0.074) was **adjacency-inflated** ~3× by a session-cumulative-`.stats` contamination the R4 replay caught (bead `xafk`) — now **corrected** in the decontaminated `routing-analysis-v3` (mean-centred Δ +0.330; the concept→routing structure survives at p = 0.0001); the glm-edrop `x_f` amendment to consume v3 remains a pre-freeze step. **The R4 offline replay has since resolved kernel-specificity deflationarily** (Fable, GPT-5.6 review-gate pending, ~$0 CPU): kernel-conditioned expert selection is only **+2.6% vs the global-hot deflator — below the registered +10% margin, so kernel-specificity is ABSENT at margin**, while an oracle confirms +19.8% exploitable structure exists and the kernel-vs-generic-embedding head-to-head is underpowered/unresolved (p = 0.094, thin 1–2-prompt data).
- **Built / in build:** KaE / F1-K (Kernel-as-Expert on GLM-5.2 — signed off [#28], driver LAUNCH-SAFE, but **inventory-limited at $0 build → authorized powered rebuild on the large kernel [#33]**, now building explications — below), the GLM-5.2 **expert-drop** efficiency track ([#27](https://github.com/jeswr/kernel-of-truth/issues/27) = A redirect — below), the large-kernel scale track (110k WordNet base, 2,404 F1-K-eligible concepts — below), and the sparq upstream PRs.

### Key findings so far

- Kernel-authored content can be externally useful: an aligned answer key plus cheap deterministic verify-retry lifted a 135M model +0.25 on external gold *(measured, verdict-grade; f2b-transfer, DECONF-B)*.
- The measured value sits in the content, not in kernel-specific runtime structure — and static kernel-style prompting showed no positive sign at its (invalidated) test *(measured, deflationary; DECONF-B, CASC-0′)*.
- Deterministic rules machinery is genuinely non-inert: it computes correct entailed answers that lookup cannot, exactly and at ~$0 *(measured certificate; RULES-1)*. Whether that helps a *model* remains **unmeasured after three instrument failures** (void → host-frame → verify-retry-vacuous); the host-integration question has proven hard to instrument at this scale, not hard to answer.
- Hard hand-authored typing looks unsound (~0.39) and soft imported typing is the leading candidate repair — a GO-shaped, unlicensed signal; both readings are LLM-proxy-provisional pending human gold *(g2, g2-import)*.
- The efficiency seam now has real physics on the record: frontier-scale MoE decode is disk-bound, and a trivial kernel-free expert-drop already buys ~2× — every kernel-guided compression or residency claim must henceforth beat that bar, not a no-drop baseline *(measured, measurement-only; GLM-5.2 P0 probe)*.
- Concept identity separates a frontier router's fingerprints under the registered test (p = 0.0001, survives clean re-test) — and the R4 offline replay now finds **kernel-specific** routing exploitation **absent at the registered +10% margin** (M_kernel +2.6% vs global-hot; oracle +19.8% confirms structure exists), the same structure-unshown trap that capped the content-not-structure results, now recurring on the efficiency side *(measured, R4 replay, GPT-5.6 review-gate pending; GLM-5.2 P0 probe)*.
- The first structure-*sensitive* instrument is validated but stopped at adequacy: sense-split typing shows a real, exploratory, *break*-only advantage that could not be confirmatorily tested because its anomaly-gold construct fails on real corpus objects *(measured kill + exploratory census; engine-inference E0)*.
- Plainly: **both theses remain open.** Nothing yet shows a kernel-equipped small model matching a larger one, kernel-*specific* necessity, natural-language input reach, or favourable end-to-end economics. CORRECTNESS and EFFICIENCY are each INCONCLUSIVE-PENDING.

## Method

Multi-agent research under an empirical-honesty mandate: every load-bearing claim cited; `[established]` / `[claimed]` / `[speculative]` tags kept distinct; negative results reported; the programme is explicitly designed to find out whether the hypothesis is *seriously* right, partially right, or wrong.
