CONCEPT: gravel

Definitional text:
Wiktionary (rev 91022283/c1ffe470-79f4-11f1-85ce-a1778772daf2):
(noun) Small fragments of rock, used for laying on the beds of roads and railways, and as ballast.
(noun) A type or grade of small rocks, differentiated by mineral type, size range, or other characteristics.
(noun) A particle from 2 to 64 mm in diameter, following the Wentworth scale.
(noun) Kidney stones, or nephrolithiasis
(verb) To apply a layer of gravel to the surface of a road, etc.
(verb) To puzzle or annoy.
(verb) To run (as a ship) upon the gravel or beach; to run aground; to (cause to) be grounded in gravel or sand.
(verb) To hurt or lame (a horse) by gravel or similar material lodged between the shoe and foot.
Wikipedia (rev 1354160096): Gravel is a loose aggregation of rock fragments. Gravel occurs naturally on Earth as a result of sedimentary and erosive geological processes; it is also produced in large quantities commercially as crushed stone.
