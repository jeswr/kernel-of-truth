Wiktionary (rev 90307272/db1f6e40-79f3-11f1-b1e4-032e0c72bdbd):
(noun) A daughter of the same parents as another person; a female sibling.
(noun) A female member of a religious order; especially one devoted to more active service;  a nun.
(noun) Any butterfly in the genus Adelpha, so named for the resemblance of the dark-colored wings to the black habit traditionally worn by nuns.
(noun) A senior or supervisory nurse, often in a hospital.
(verb) To strengthen (a supporting beam) by fastening a second beam alongside it.
(verb) To be sister to; to resemble closely.
Wikipedia (rev 1355096016): A sister is a woman or a girl who shares parents or a parent with another individual; a female sibling. The male counterpart is a brother. Although the term typically refers to a familial relationship, it is sometimes used endearingly to refer to non-familial relationships. A full sister is a first-degree relative.
