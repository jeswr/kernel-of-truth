Definitional text:
Wiktionary (rev 91415820/d7a1ead0-79f4-11f1-9fed-31e946f287c3):
(verb) To rest in a state of reduced consciousness.
(verb) To have sexual intercourse (see sleep with).
(verb) To accommodate in beds.
(verb) To be careless, inattentive, or unconcerned; not to be vigilant; to live thoughtlessly.
(noun) The state of reduced consciousness during which a human or animal rests in a daily rhythm.
(noun) An act or instance of sleeping.
(noun) A night.
(noun) Rheum, crusty or gummy discharge found in the corner of the eyes after waking, whether real or a figurative objectification of sleep (in the sense of reduced consciousness).
Wikipedia (rev 1360078399): Sleep is a state of reduced mental and physical activity in which consciousness is altered and certain sensory activity is inhibited. During sleep, there is a marked decrease in muscle activity and interactions with the surrounding environment. While sleep differs from wakefulness in terms of the ability to react to stimuli, it still involves active brain patterns, making it more reactive than a coma or disorders of consciousness.
