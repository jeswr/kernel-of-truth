CONCEPT: money

Definitional text:
Wiktionary (rev 91373492/d3ba5060-79f4-11f1-8ec0-5b575308089a):
(noun) A generally accepted means of exchange.
(noun) A currency maintained by a state or other entity which can guarantee its value (such as a monetary union). (Should we delete this sense?)
(noun) Hard cash in the form of banknotes and coins, as opposed to cheques, credit cards, or credit more generally.
(noun) The total value of liquid assets available for an individual or other economic unit, such as cash and bank deposits.
(adjective) Cool; excellent.
Wikipedia (rev 1361384199): Money is any item or verifiable record that is generally accepted as payment for goods and services and repayment of debts, such as taxes, in a particular country or socio-economic context. The primary functions which distinguish money are: medium of exchange, a unit of account, a store of value and sometimes, a standard of deferred payment.
