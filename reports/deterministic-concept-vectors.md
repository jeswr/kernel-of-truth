# Deterministic Concept Vectors: A Literature Survey

**Kernel of Truth programme — research report**
**Question:** Does a principled, deterministic, training-free map from typed trees/graphs over a small closed vocabulary (~65 NSM primes + concept references, ≤32 clauses, role slots, operators, coreference) to fixed-dimension vectors exist, with provable injectivity/near-injectivity, meaningful similarity structure, compositionality, and decodability?
**Date:** 2026-07-07. Sources verified against primary texts where marked; capacity theorems extracted from the original papers (Plate 1995; Thomas, Dasgupta & Rosing JAIR 2021; Frady, Kleyko & Sommer 2018; Kleyko et al. CSUR survey Parts I–II).

**Short answer:** Yes, with qualifications. The four desiderata are jointly achievable *within the stated size caps* at dimensions in the low tens of thousands — the VSA capacity literature quantifies this precisely — but three structural trade-offs are unavoidable: (1) decodability of deep recursive structure requires an external cleanup memory (a lexicon of known sub-vectors); the vector alone is not self-decodable at depth; (2) the similarity you provably get is *structural-overlap* (graph-kernel-like) similarity, not semantic similarity — a single deep NOT flips meaning while moving the vector ~1%; (3) worst-case *deterministic* guarantees (as opposed to "with high probability over a fixed random seed, verifiable offline") cost roughly a quadratic rather than linear dimension in structure size. None of these kills the programme; all three dictate architecture.

---

## 0. Framing: what "deterministic" and "injective" can mean here

Two clarifications the literature forces on us:

- **Determinism is cheap; the guarantees are the issue.** Every "random" construction below (random codebooks, random projections, feature hashing) becomes deterministic by fixing the PRNG seed. The guarantees are then "with high probability over the draw" — but for a *fixed* draw the relevant properties (pairwise incoherence of the codebook, coherence of a sensing matrix) are *checkable offline in polynomial time*. So the real dichotomy is: (a) probabilistic constructions with linear dimension whose properties are certified post hoc, vs (b) explicit worst-case constructions (orthogonal/Welch-bound codes, deterministic RIP matrices) with stronger guarantees but quadratically worse dimension (the "square-root bottleneck", §5.4). For a closed vocabulary of a few hundred atoms, option (b) is actually free: **any vocabulary smaller than the dimension D can be assigned exactly orthonormal codes** (e.g. rows of a Hadamard or DFT matrix), making the atom-level guarantees exact rather than probabilistic. Only the open-ended concept-reference lexicon (10^4–10^6 entries > D) needs quasi-orthogonal codes with coherence μ ≈ √(ln m / D), and the Welch bound μ ≥ √((m−D)/(D(m−1))) tells you exactly what is achievable [established; Welch 1974].

- **Injectivity per se is trivial; robust injectivity is the real constraint.** Reals have infinite precision, so an injective map from any countable set of structures into R^1 exists (Gödel numbering). The meaningful requirement is injectivity *with margin* under finite precision and noise: distinct structures map to vectors separated by a decodable margin. That is exactly what VSA capacity theory prices: a D-dimensional robust vector holds ~0.2–0.5 bits per dimension of recoverable structure (§1.3), so the size caps translate directly into a dimension requirement (§7.2). This confirms and *quantifies* the prior that fixed-dimension vectors cannot be robustly injective over unbounded structures — and shows the caps put the explication space comfortably inside the feasible region.

## 1. Vector Symbolic Architectures / Hyperdimensional Computing

### 1.1 The framework [established]

VSA/HDC represents symbols as high-dimensional vectors ("hypervectors") and builds structures with three operations: **superposition** (elementwise addition; result is *similar* to its inputs), **binding** (circular convolution in Plate's HRR; elementwise multiplication in Gayler's MAP; XOR in Kanerva's Binary Spatter Codes; result is *dissimilar* to its inputs but preserves distances: bind(x,a)·bind(x,b) ≈ a·b), and **permutation** (for order/position; ρ(x) is dissimilar to x). Key primary sources:

- Plate, "Holographic Reduced Representations", *IEEE Trans. Neural Networks* 6(3):623–641, 1995; book: *Holographic Reduced Representation*, CSLI 2003. Circular convolution binding z = x ⊛ y with approximate-inverse decoding y ≈ x* ⊛ z followed by **cleanup** (nearest-neighbour against an item memory). Frames, sequences, and *recursive* structures (his "reduced representations") are all constructed; a role-filler frame is Σᵢ roleᵢ ⊛ fillerᵢ.
- Kanerva, "Hyperdimensional Computing: An Introduction…", *Cognitive Computation* 1(2):139–159, 2009. Concentration of measure: random points in {±1}^D are nearly orthogonal; a superposition of a few dozen items remains recognisably similar to each.
- Smolensky's TPR is the exact (uncompressed) ancestor — see §2. HRR is literally a lossy compression of the tensor product (circular convolution = projection of the outer product onto D dimensions) [established; Plate 1995 §II].
- Surveys: Kleyko, Rachkovskij, Osipov & Rahimi, *ACM Computing Surveys*, Part I (55(6), 2022; arXiv:2111.06077) and Part II (55(9), 2023; arXiv:2112.15424). Gayler, "VSAs answer Jackendoff's challenges", 2003 (arXiv:cs/0412059) is the classic argument that VSA handles variable binding, coreference, and recursive structure — precisely our requirements list.

An existence proof at system scale: Eliasmith's Semantic Pointer Architecture drives Spaun, a 2.5M-neuron functioning cognitive model, entirely on HRR-style structured vectors with cleanup memories (Eliasmith et al., *Science* 338:1202–1205, 2012) [established as an engineering artifact].

### 1.2 Capacity: the quantitative core [established]

These are the theorems that price our size caps.

- **Plate 1995, Appendix B** (extracted from the paper): the number of role-filler pairs k storable in one n-dimensional convolution trace and reliably recognised against a codebook of m items with total error probability q satisfies approximately **k ≳ n / (16 ln(m/q))** (conservative lower bound), with numerical solutions of his capacity equation well approximated by **n ≈ 4.5·(k+0.7)·ln(m/(30q⁴))** (constants as printed; the robust content is *n = Θ(k·ln(m/q))* — linear in the number of stored bindings, logarithmic in codebook size and error rate). Deep structures decode slightly worse than shallow ones of the same term count because the variance of long convolution products grows; **using unitary vectors (unit-magnitude Fourier components) eliminates this depth penalty** [Plate 1995 §VIII-C, §IX — directly relevant to our ≤32-clause nested trees].
- **Frady, Kleyko & Sommer, *Neural Computation* 30(6):1449–1513, 2018 (arXiv:1803.00412)** (extracted from the paper): exact (non-asymptotic) theory of retrieval from superpositions. Retrieval SNR is **s = √(N/M)** for M items superposed in N dimensions. Total recoverable information: Plate's original analysis guarantees ≥0.16 bits/dimension; their exact theory shows the true optimum lies in the *low-fidelity* regime and reaches **≈0.4 bits/dimension for a 27-symbol alphabet and >0.5 bits/dimension for large alphabets**; the no-superposition limit (M=1) achieves the optimal 1 bit/dimension; asymptotic high-fidelity capacity is 1/(4 ln 2) ≈ 0.36 bits/dim. **The moment anything is superposed, crosstalk (not noise) is the binding constraint.**
- **Thomas, Dasgupta & Rosing, "A Theoretical Perspective on Hyperdimensional Computing", *JAIR* 72:215–249, 2021 (arXiv:2010.07426)** (theorems extracted from the paper): a non-asymptotic, sub-Gaussian-codebook treatment.
  - *Thm 2:* set membership decodes perfectly by thresholding ⟨φ(a), φ(S)⟩ ≥ ½L² if the codebook is 1/(2s)-incoherent (s = |S|). Via worst-case incoherence this needs **d = O(s² ln m)**; via their subset-incoherence property (Thm 7) whp decoding needs only **d = O(s ln m)** — they note this matches Plate 2003, Gallant & Okaywe 2013, and Frady et al. 2018.
  - *Thm 9 (similarity):* for sets S, S′: |S∩S′| − ss′μ ≤ ⟨φ(S),φ(S′)⟩/L² ≤ |S∩S′| + ss′μ. **The inner product of two encodings provably estimates the overlap of their feature multisets.** This is the formal basis for "similar definitions → nearby vectors" — with the caveat that "similar" means *feature overlap* (§7.1).
  - *Thms 14–16:* elementwise-product binding and permutation preserve incoherence with only logarithmic penalty in (alphabet × roles); Thm 15 extends the similarity estimate to bound role-filler structures.
  - *Thm 10 + Lemmas 11–13:* noise robustness — decoding tolerates additive noise up to a constant fraction of the signal margin, with explicit trade-off between incoherence (dimension) and noise budget. They also identify the superposition encoding as a real-valued **Bloom filter** — the right mental model: a VSA vector *is* a linear sketch of the structure's feature multiset.

### 1.3 Decoding: cleanup memories and resonator networks [established, with sharp limits]

- Unbinding + cleanup recovers one factor when *all other factors are known*. Recovering an unknown combination is combinatorial. The Kleyko survey's own Open Issues list (Part II §4.3, quoted from the text) is blunt: "*Recovering element HVs from compositional HVs… for most of the HDC/VSA models, knowledge of all but one bound HV is required. This makes the recovery problem combinatorial.*"
- **Resonator networks** (Frady, Kent, Olshausen & Sommer, *Neural Computation* 32(12):2311–2331 and Kent, Frady, Sommer & Olshausen, 32(12):2332–2388, 2020; arXiv:1906.11684) solve exactly this: given z = a⊙b⊙c with each factor from a known codebook, iterated superposition-search factorises it. **Operational capacity (largest reliably solvable factorisation problem, i.e. product of codebook sizes) scales quadratically with dimension N** and is ~2 orders of magnitude beyond ALS/gradient methods [established by large-scale simulation, not closed-form proof]. Follow-ons: factorizers for sparse block codes (Langenegger et al., arXiv:2303.13957), noise-aided factorizers (arXiv:2412.00354), FactorHD (arXiv:2507.12366).
- **Graph recovery demonstrated end-to-end:** GrapHD (Poduval et al., *Frontiers in Neuroscience* 16:757125, 2022) encodes a whole graph as one hypervector (superposition of bound node-pair vectors) and *reconstructs the edge set* by exhaustive similarity queries; e.g. a 30-node/150-edge graph is recovered under 10% noise at **D ≈ 6k ≈ 6000** dimensions — i.e. ~40 dims/edge, consistent with the 0.2–0.5 bits/dim capacity arithmetic. This is the closest existing artifact to "decode the structure from the vector".
- **Improved binding operators:** unitary HRR vectors give exact inverses (Plate 1995); Vector-derived Transformation Binding (Gosmann & Eliasmith, *Neural Computation* 31(5):849–869, 2019) improves deep/stack encoding; Ganesan et al., "Learning with HRRs", NeurIPS 2021, show a complex-unit-magnitude projection makes retrieval error linear-capacity in practice — **1024 bound pairs recognisable in 256 complex dimensions at <3% error** (recognition, not full recovery).
- **Sparse VSAs:** Rachkovskij & Kussul's Context-Dependent Thinning (*Neural Computation* 13(2):411–452, 2001) is notable because its binding *preserves similarity to the bound components* — directly attacking the binding/similarity tension (survey Open Issue: "*the results of the binding operation are not similar as soon as a single input HV is dissimilar*"). Sparse block codes with exact binding: Frady, Kleyko & Sommer, IEEE TNNLS 2021 (arXiv:2009.06734); Laiho et al. 2015.

### 1.4 Continuous values: Fractional Power Encoding / VFA [established]

Fractional binding z^x (x ∈ R) encodes continuous quantities; inner products then realise a translation-invariant similarity kernel — FPE is random Fourier features in disguise. Frady, Kleyko, Kymn, Olshausen & Sommer, "Computing on Functions Using Randomized Vector Representations" (arXiv:2109.03429; NICE 2022) formalise this as Vector Function Architecture: the vector space is an RKHS and VSA ops are well-defined function-space ops. Komer et al.'s Spatial Semantic Pointers (CogSci 2019) are the applied instance; improved decoding of FPE: arXiv:2412.00488. Relevant to us only at the margins (degree/intensity slots — e.g. VERY/MORE gradation), but it means graded attributes can live in the same algebra with a *known kernel*.

### 1.5 Knowledge-graph note: HolE [established, but trained]

Nickel, Rosasco & Poggio, "Holographic Embeddings of Knowledge Graphs" (AAAI 2016, arXiv:1510.04935) score triples with Plate's circular correlation — evidence that HRR composition supports relational structure at web scale — but the embeddings are *learned*; HolE was later shown equivalent to ComplEx (Hayashi & Shimbo, arXiv:1702.05563). Useful as a composition-operator precedent, not as a training-free method.

### 1.6 What breaks at our scale

- 65 primes + roles/operators/indices as atoms: **fine** — vocabulary < D means exactly orthonormal atomic codes are available (§0); all incoherence-based bounds tighten.
- ≤32 clauses ≈ 100–200 bound terms per explication: **inside capacity** at D ~ 10⁴ (arithmetic in §7.2).
- Deep nesting: variance growth is fixed by unitary/phasor codes [Plate], **but recursive decoding compounds error across levels** — reliable deep decoding requires cleanup after *every* unbinding, i.e. an item memory containing every intermediate subtree vector. Self-contained decoding of an arbitrary unknown deep tree from one vector, with no lexicon, has no positive result in this literature; the resonator-network results cover flat products of 2–4 factors, not unbounded recursion.
- Coreference: no special obstacle — indexed referents are one more binding (GrapHD handles arbitrary edge sets; Gayler 2003 discusses variable binding explicitly).

## 2. Tensor Product Representations (Smolensky)

- **Smolensky, *Artificial Intelligence* 46:159–216, 1990** [established]: structure = Σᵢ rᵢ ⊗ fᵢ. With **linearly independent role vectors, unbinding via the dual basis is *exact*** — perfect, provable decodability with zero crosstalk; with orthonormal roles the dual basis is the basis itself. Injectivity is exact. Similarity is the sum of role-wise filler similarities. Graceful saturation when roles exceed independence. This is the *only* construction in the survey with exact everything — and its cost is explicit: the representation of a depth-h binary tree needs a tensor of order h (dimension (role-dim)^h · filler-dim), i.e. **dimension exponential in depth**. HRR/VSA is precisely the lossy fixed-dimension compression of TPR, trading exactness for the capacity bounds of §1.2.
- **Modern neural use** [established empirically]: McCoy, Linzen, Dunbar & Smolensky, "RNNs Implicitly Implement Tensor Product Representations" (ICLR 2019, arXiv:1812.08718): trained seq2seq autoencoder representations are well approximated by TPRs with interpretable role schemes — *but* representations from standard sentence encoders (InferSent, Skip-thought…) are approximated about as well by **bag-of-words role schemes**, i.e. trained encoders barely encode structure. This is evidence *against* expecting trained embeddings to supply the structural sensitivity we need, and indirectly *for* building it in by construction. TPR-RNN (Schlag & Schmidhuber, NeurIPS 2018) and TP-Transformer (Schlag et al., arXiv:1910.06611) inject TPR operations into trained nets; Smolensky et al.'s "neurocompositional computing" (AI Magazine 2022) is the programmatic statement.
- **Verdict for us:** TPR is the right *exact* semantics for shallow role-filler layers (a clause: predicate + ≤6 role slots — order-2 tensor, exactly decodable); it cannot be the whole-tree representation (exponential blow-up). The natural design is TPR-exact within a clause, compressed binding across clauses/depth (§7.3, Construction B).

## 3. Compositional distributional semantics (DisCoCat)

- Coecke, Sadrzadeh & Clark, "Mathematical Foundations for a Compositional Distributional Model of Meaning" (2010, arXiv:1003.4394) [established as mathematics]: pregroup grammar derivations interpreted as linear maps on tensor products of word representations; sentence meaning = deterministic contraction of word tensors along the parse. The *composition* is deterministic and principled (a functor from grammar to FVect); the *word tensors* are learned from corpora — so DisCoCat is "deterministic given the lexicon", like us.
- **Scaling limits** [established]: a transitive verb is an order-3 tensor (n³ parameters), ditransitives order-4+; learning and storing higher-order tensors is the acknowledged bottleneck (Grefenstette & Sadrzadeh, EMNLP 2011; Kartsaklis et al., lambeq, arXiv:2110.04236). The quantum-NLP line (Lorenz et al., *Quantum Machine Intelligence* 2024) explicitly motivates quantum hardware by the *classical intractability* of the tensor semantics [claimed]. Logical words, quantifiers, and negation remain awkward (density-matrix and other extensions exist but are not settled). Empirically, wins over additive baselines on disambiguation tasks are real but modest (Concrete Models and Empirical Evaluations, *Computational Linguistics* 41(1):71–118, 2015).
- **Verdict for us:** DisCoCat validates the category-theoretic shape of "grammar → linear-algebraic composition, deterministically" and warns concretely: *do not represent operators/predicates as full higher-order tensors*; use structured/low-rank maps (VSA binding is exactly a parameter-free such choice — circular convolution is a fixed bilinear map with n, not n³, degrees of freedom).

## 4. Gärdenfors conceptual spaces

- Gärdenfors, *Conceptual Spaces: The Geometry of Thought* (MIT Press, 2000): concepts = convex regions in spaces of quality dimensions; prototypes + Voronoi tessellations yield convex categories; convexity ⇒ generalisation-by-interpolation [established as theory].
- Computational instantiations exist but are hand-modelled, not derived from symbolic definitions: Bechberger & Kühnberger's formalization (arXiv:1706.06366) and implementation (arXiv:1707.05165) using fuzzy star-shaped sets with computable intersection/union/projection; earlier formalizations by Aisbett & Gibbon 2001, Rickard 2007, Lewis & Lawry 2016. Quantum-inspired concept models (arXiv:2302.14822, arXiv:2401.08585) *learn* the spaces.
- **The gap:** conceptual spaces give a semantics for *similarity* (regions, betweenness, convexity) but provide **no deterministic map from a symbolic definition to a point/region** — the quality dimensions themselves are chosen empirically. Interesting bridge work: "Analogical Reasoning Within a Conceptual Hyperspace" (arXiv:2411.08684) combines VSA operations with conceptual-space semantics [claimed].
- **Verdict for us:** conceptual spaces are a target semantics (what similarity *should* mean — shared quality structure), not an encoder. If explication trees are the definitions, the VSA/WL similarity of §1/§5 is what is constructively available; conceptual-space-style semantic similarity would have to be induced by the *content* of definitions (shared primes/subtrees), which is in fact what feature-overlap similarity delivers at the terminal level.

## 5. Deterministic graph/tree → vector: kernels, hashing, canonical forms

### 5.1 Weisfeiler–Lehman and tree identity [established]

- Shervashidze, Schweitzer, van Leeuwen, Mehlhorn & Borgwardt, "Weisfeiler-Lehman Graph Kernels", *JMLR* 12:2539–2561, 2011: deterministic iterated colour refinement produces subtree features in O(edges × iterations); the kernel is the feature-multiset inner product.
- **1-WL/colour refinement identifies all trees and forests up to isomorphism** (classical; see Arvind, Köbler, Rattan & Verbitsky, "On the Power of Color Refinement", FCT 2015), and the Aho–Hopcroft–Ullman algorithm (1974) computes a **canonical form of a labelled tree in linear time**. Our explications are labelled trees plus indexed coreference edges over distinct labels; a canonical form is straightforwardly computable (canonical traversal order fixes referent indices). ⇒ **Exact injectivity at the *symbolic* level is a solved problem**; the only question is what survives the projection to fixed dimension.
- GNN ceiling: message-passing GNNs are at most 1-WL (Xu et al., GIN, ICLR 2019, arXiv:1810.00826); their injective-sum-aggregation theorem holds for countable feature spaces and *relies on unbounded precision* — with b-bit precision, simulating WL needs the precision/width priced by Aamand et al. (NeurIPS 2022). Same finite-precision moral as §0.
- **Spectral embeddings — negative result worth remembering:** Schwenk (1973): **almost all trees have a cospectral mate** — adjacency/Laplacian spectra are provably *non*-injective exactly on our structure class. Spectral graph embeddings are disqualified as identity codes [established].
- Convolution tree kernels (Collins & Duffy, NeurIPS 2001) count shared subtrees in O(|T₁||T₂|) — deterministic similarity, no fixed-dimension vector, no decoding.

### 5.2 Feature hashing and locality-sensitive hashing [established]

- Weinberger, Dasgupta, Langford, Smola & Attenberg (ICML 2009, arXiv:0902.2206): the signed hashing trick gives **E⟨φ(x),φ(x′)⟩ = ⟨x,x′⟩** with exponential tail bounds — a deterministic (fixed hash) linear sketch of an unbounded feature space into fixed D that preserves the WL kernel in expectation. WL + hashing = deterministic fixed-D graph embedding with provable similarity and quantified collision noise.
- SimHash (Charikar, STOC 2002): Pr[collision] = 1 − θ/π preserves angular similarity in O(1)-bit codes; MinHash (Broder 1997) preserves Jaccard of feature sets. Both deterministic given seeds; neither decodable.
- Johnson–Lindenstrauss: any n points survive projection to D = O(ε⁻² log n) with distances preserved to (1±ε); this is *optimal* (Larsen & Nelson, FOCS 2017). Fixing the projection matrix makes it deterministic; for our finite (capped) structure family this alone guarantees a similarity-preserving near-injective map — but with no decoding and no compositionality.

### 5.3 Cryptographic multiset hashing: exact injectivity, incrementally [established]

Bellare & Micciancio's randomize-then-combine paradigm (EUROCRYPT 1997): H(S) = ⊕_{x∈S} h(x) with ⊕ a group operation gives **incremental, order-invariant, collision-resistant set/multiset hashes** (AdHash, MuHash, LtHash); multiset security requires care (Wagner's generalized birthday attack, CRYPTO 2002, and lattice attacks force large moduli — see Clarke et al., ASIACRYPT 2003; Elliptic Curve Multiset Hash, arXiv:1601.06502; deployed LtHash, Lewi et al., IACR eprint 2019/227). Composed with the AHU canonical form this yields a 256-bit deterministic identity code for explications with cryptographic near-injectivity **and homomorphic updates** (edit a clause → update the hash without re-hashing the tree). Zero similarity structure, zero decodability — by design (avalanche).

### 5.4 Compressed sensing: provable decoding of sparse structure [established]

The pivotal decodability tool outside VSA. If a structure is an s-sparse vector x over a feature dictionary of size N, then a fixed matrix Φ ∈ R^{D×N} with the Restricted Isometry Property allows **exact recovery of x from Φx by ℓ₁ minimisation with D = O(s log(N/s))** (Candès & Tao 2005/2006; Donoho 2006; simple proof: Baraniuk, Davenport, DeVore & Wakin, *Constructive Approximation* 2008). Fixed-seed random ±1 matrices satisfy RIP whp; certifying RIP is NP-hard, but coherence-based certificates guarantee recovery of s < (1 + 1/μ)/2 sparse vectors (Donoho & Elad, PNAS 2003; Tropp, IEEE-IT 2004) and coherence *is* checkable. **Deterministic worst-case RIP constructions hit the square-root bottleneck: D = Ω(s²)** (barely broken by Bourgain et al. 2011, s^{2−ε}) — this is the precise price of insisting on worst-case rather than fixed-seed-verified guarantees.

Directly on point: **Arora, Khodak, Saunshi & Vodrahalli, "A Compressed Sensing View of Unsupervised Text Embeddings, Bag-of-n-Grams, and LSTMs" (ICLR 2018)** prove that additive/LSTM text embeddings are information-preserving linear measurements of bag-of-n-gram vectors — **the n-gram content is provably recoverable from low-dimensional embeddings** (code: NLPrinceton/sparse_recovery), and pretrained word vectors form *better-than-random* sensing matrices. This is the published existence proof that "sentence-embedding-like" fixed-D vectors can be *decodable* representations of discrete structure. Replace n-grams with WL subtree features and this is our Construction C (§7.3).

## 6. Training-free / fixed-seed embeddings

- **Wieting & Kiela, "No Training Required: Exploring Random Encoders for Sentence Classification" (ICLR 2019, arXiv:1901.10444)** [established empirically]: random projections/LSTMs/ESNs over pretrained word vectors nearly match trained sentence encoders on SentEval. Cuts both ways: (for us) untrained structure encoders are not handicapped; (against everyone) most of what sentence embeddings measure is word content, not structure — consistent with McCoy et al.'s bag-of-words finding (§2). "Meaningful similarity" in the trained-embedding world is mostly lexical overlap already.
- **Tree Echo State Networks** (Gallicchio & Micheli, *Neurocomputing* 101:319–337, 2013) [established]: untrained contractive recursive reservoirs deterministically embed trees; the echo-state (contractivity) condition **provably induces Markovian/suffix bias — root-adjacent structure dominates, deep differences are exponentially attenuated** (cf. Tino & Hammer on architectural bias). A clean negative lesson: *contractive* deterministic encoders cannot be near-injective in depth; VSA's unitary/norm-preserving binding is the escape (nothing is attenuated; everything competes as crosstalk instead — which capacity theory then prices).
- Deterministic aggregation baselines (SIF etc.) inherit trained word vectors; pure fixed-seed pipelines (random codebook + VSA ops, random projection + hashing) are the genuinely training-free class, and everything provable about them is already covered by §§1, 5.

## 7. Synthesis

### 7.1 The trilemma

The literature organises cleanly around a three-way tension. Pick the guarantee you make exact, and the others degrade:

| | Injectivity | Similarity | Decodability | Dimension |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Canonical form + crypto hash (§5.3) | cryptographic | none | none | 256 bits |
| WL features + JL/hashing (§5.1–5.2) | whp, margin | kernel = feature overlap | none (sketch only) | O(ε⁻² log n) |
| WL features + compressed sensing (§5.4) | exact on s-sparse | ≈ feature overlap | **exact ℓ₁ recovery** | O(s log N) whp / Ω(s²) worst-case |
| TPR (§2) | exact | role-wise | **exact unbinding** | exponential in depth |
| VSA/HRR + cleanup (§1) | whp, margin | ≈ weighted feature overlap | recursive unbind + cleanup + resonator | O(s ln m), constants known |

Two of the desiderata deserve honesty:

- **"Similar definitions → nearby vectors" is provable only for *structural-overlap* similarity** (Thomas Thm 9/15; Plate's NIPS 1993 analogical-similarity results: HRR dot products track shared objects/relations and some structural arrangement). Semantic similarity diverges from structural overlap exactly where it matters most: one NOT/antonym flip deep in a 32-clause tree moves the vector by ~1/s of its norm while inverting the meaning. No surveyed construction solves this; it must be handled *above* the encoder (canonicalisation, polarity-weighted encodings, or accepting that the kernel provides identity+overlap and meaning-level similarity is computed relationally).
- **Decodability of deep recursion is never self-contained.** Exact recovery exists for flat sparse structures (compressed sensing) and single-level role-filler frames (TPR); recursive VSA decoding needs a cleanup memory holding every decodable intermediate. For the Kernel of Truth this is arguably a feature, not a bug: the kernel *is* a closed lexicon, so the cleanup memory is simply the kernel itself (every defined concept's canonical vector), and decoding = recursive unbind + nearest-neighbour against the kernel. But it must be a design commitment from day one.

### 7.2 What the caps buy: the arithmetic

Explication budget: ≤32 clauses × ~3–6 bound terms (predicate, role fillers, operator flags, referent indices, parent link) ⇒ **s ≈ 100–200 bound terms**; atom codebook: 65 primes + ~50 roles/operators + ~64 clause/referent indices ≈ **m₀ ≈ 200 closed atoms** (⇒ exactly orthogonal codes available for D > m₀) plus a concept-reference lexicon **m ≈ 10⁴–10⁶** (quasi-orthogonal, μ ≈ √(ln m / D)).

- **Structure entropy:** ~s·log₂(m) ≈ 150 × 13–20 bits ≈ **2–4 kbit** per explication (generous).
- **Robust-capacity requirement** at 0.2–0.5 bits/dim (Frady 2018): **D ≈ 4,000–20,000** for full decodability. Cross-checks: Plate's formula with k = 200, ln(m/q) ≈ 20–28 gives n ≈ 18k–25k for very low error; GrapHD empirically recovered 150 edges at D = 6,000; Thomas' d = O(s ln m) with certified constants lands in the same decade.
- **Recognition/equality only:** 256 bits (crypto) or D ≈ ε⁻² log(#structures) ≈ a few thousand for margin-based distinguishability of astronomically many (2^~2000) capped explications — sphere-packing makes fixed small D compatible with *any* finite capped family, which is exactly what the caps buy on the injectivity front.
- **Deterministic worst-case decoding** (coherence-certified, square-root bottleneck): D = Ω(s²) ≈ 10⁴–4×10⁴ — inconvenient but not fatal.

**Conclusion of the arithmetic: at D ≈ 8k–32k (a 16–64 kB fp16 vector per concept; 3–6 GB for a 10⁵-concept kernel), the capped explication space sits inside every relevant capacity bound, with published constants.** The prior — no robust injectivity over unbounded structures — is correct and quantified: capacity is linear in D (0.2–0.5 bits/dim), so structures beyond ~D/2 bound terms *must* collide or lose decodability; the 32-clause cap is what keeps s·ln m ≪ D.

### 7.3 The 2–3 most promising constructions

**A. Canonical form + homomorphic multiset hash (identity layer).** AHU-canonicalise the explication (linear time; coreference fixed by canonical traversal), hash clauses with LtHash-style randomize-then-combine. *Provable:* collision-resistant injectivity; O(1) equality; incremental updates under editing. *Failure:* no similarity, no decoding. *Role:* the kernel's identity/versioning code alongside (not instead of) the geometric vector — also the honest baseline any geometric claim of "near-injectivity" must be measured against.

**B. Two-level TPR/VSA encoder with kernel-as-cleanup ("structured unitary HRR") — the primary candidate.** Clause level: exact TPR over *orthonormal* role/prime codes (deterministic Hadamard/DFT codebook; exact unbinding, zero crosstalk within a clause). Tree level: unitary circular-convolution binding (no depth-variance growth [Plate §VIII-C]) + permutation for clause position + superposition across clauses; concept references resolve to the referenced concept's own canonical vector (compositionality by construction); every defined concept's vector is stored in the kernel lexicon = cleanup memory. *Provable:* whp margin injectivity at D = O(s ln m) with the §7.2 constants, certifiable offline for the fixed codebook; similarity = weighted role-filler overlap (Thomas Thm 9/15, Plate 1993); decoding by recursive unbinding + cleanup, with resonator networks (capacity ∝ D²) for multi-factor terms; noise robustness with explicit margins (Thomas Thm 10). *Failure modes:* decode error compounds with depth between cleanups; similarity is syntactic (NOT-flip problem); superposition weighting is a free design parameter that silently determines whether root or leaf differences dominate similarity (the TreeESN suffix-bias lesson in reverse).

**C. WL-subtree features + fixed sensing matrix (compressed-sensing decoder).** Expand the explication into its multiset of WL-coloured subtree features (deterministic; injective on trees since colour refinement identifies trees); encode the s-sparse feature vector with a fixed (seed-verified or coherence-certified) Φ; decode by ℓ₁/OMP recovery, then reassemble the tree from its subtree multiset (unique by canonical colours). *Provable:* exact recovery at D = O(s log N) (Candès–Tao; Arora et al. 2018 is the working precedent with n-grams); inner products ≈ WL kernel (Weinberger 2009). *Failure modes:* the feature dictionary is open-ended (hash-truncate it → quantifiable collision noise); worst-case-deterministic certification costs D = Ω(s²); recovery is batch optimisation, not an algebraic unbind — less natural inside a neural architecture. *Note:* B and C are secretly the same object (a VSA superposition **is** a linear sketch of the feature multiset — Thomas et al.'s Bloom-filter identification); C is the right formal lens for proving B's decodability, B is the right operational form.

### 7.4 The biggest theoretical obstacle, stated honestly

It is **not** injectivity — the caps plus D ≈ 10⁴ put us an order of magnitude inside published capacity bounds, and cryptographic hashing supplies exact identity for free. The binding constraint is the **conjunction of similarity with decodable compositional depth**:

1. **Robust information ceiling (the quantified prior):** recoverable structure is ≤ ~0.5 bits/dimension (Frady 2018) — fixed-D vectors are *provably not* injective-with-margin beyond s ≈ D/(2 ln m) bound terms. The 32-clause cap is not a convenience; it is the load-bearing assumption. Anything that expands effective structure size (inlining referenced concepts' definitions instead of pointing to their vectors, unrolling coreference) burns the budget quadratically fast.
2. **Similarity–binding incompatibility:** binding must randomise (else no capacity), so similarity lives only in superposition overlap — hence syntactic, and blind to polarity/scope inversions that dominate meaning. The survey lists this as an open problem in the field (Kleyko Part II §4.3 "Similarity"); partial mitigations (CDT sparse binding, polarity-aware feature weighting) exist, no solution does.
3. **No self-contained deep decoding:** every positive decoding result (cleanup, resonator, compressed sensing) presupposes a known codebook/dictionary; recursive structures additionally need intermediate cleanup, i.e. the lexicon. The "kernel" must therefore be architected as *vectors + lexicon-as-cleanup-memory*, and the claim "the structure is recoverable from the vector" must always be stated as "recoverable given the kernel". Worst-case deterministic guarantees for all of the above cost D = Ω(s²) (Welch bound / square-root bottleneck); at our s that is still feasible, which is the single luckiest fact the caps buy us.

## 8. Evidence against, collected

- Trained encoders don't encode structure (McCoy et al. ICLR 2019: bag-of-words TPR approximations suffice for sentence encoders); random encoders match trained ones (Wieting & Kiela ICLR 2019) — the field's "sentence similarity" is largely lexical, so no existing empirical benchmark validates *structural* similarity as useful.
- Spectral embeddings provably fail on trees (Schwenk 1973: almost all trees have cospectral mates).
- Contractive deterministic recursive encoders provably suffer suffix/root bias (TreeESN Markovian characterisation).
- DisCoCat's exact tensor semantics did not scale classically (order-k tensors; the community's escape hatches are dimension reduction and quantum hardware); empirical wins over additive baselines are modest.
- VSA recovery is combinatorial in general and is listed as an open issue by the field's own survey (Kleyko et al. Part II §4.3); resonator-network capacity is established by simulation, not proof, and covers flat factorisations only.
- HRR's history in ML: largely dormant 1995–2020; the NeurIPS 2021 revival needed a numerical fix (unit-magnitude projection) to make gradients and retrieval behave — the vanilla operator is finicky in practice.
- Conceptual spaces, the best-developed theory of *semantic* geometric similarity, provides no constructive symbolic→geometric map at all.
