## 1. Findings (tagged)

### 1.1 The exact invariances of transformer surgery — the load-bearing core

A wrong invariance claim breaks H-DD, so this section carries the heaviest verification. **All invariance-condition claims in the draft verified at source; one draft self-correction confirmed correct.**

- **F1 — Computational invariance is exact but conditional (SliceGPT).** An orthogonal transform **Q of the residual stream** can be absorbed into adjacent weight matrices leaving the network function unchanged, **provided the inter-block normalisation is parameter-free RMSNorm**; LayerNorm networks must first be *converted* (mean-subtraction + scale absorbed into neighbours). Deleting PCA-ranked rows/columns after absorption (the "slice") is **NOT** function-preserving — it minimises the **L2 reconstruction error of the calibration activations**, a proxy for, not identical to, downstream behaviour. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2401.15024; retention numbers + method confirmed at abstract; slicing objective + RMSNorm requirement corroborated by full-text reviews).
- **F2 — SliceGPT uses per-block Q_ℓ, NOT one global residual-stream Q (draft self-correction CONFIRMED).** Because "the signals at different blocks of the network were not aligned," SliceGPT applies a **different orthogonal Q_ℓ at each block** and inserts explicit **bridge matrices Q_{ℓ−1}ᵀQ_ℓ into each residual/skip connection** — small D×D operations that "**cannot be pre-computed**." A single global Q is licit only in the fully-merged setting (QuaRot). **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2401.15024 §3.3; verified via full-text review sources — the draft's correction of its own earlier "one global Q" statement is right). *Consequence:* H-DD's "one normalise step" is wrong twice over — the licit schemes are (a) global-Q only after an explicit re-parameterisation, or (b) per-block Q_ℓ **with** residual bridges. Omitting the bridges changes the function.
- **F3 — Rotation absorption has FUNDAMENTAL non-absorbable sites (QuaRot).** Verified verbatim at HTML source: the identity **RMSNorm(X)=RMSNorm(XQᵀ)Q** licenses residual-stream rotation absorption into W_q/W_k/W_v/W_up/W_gate (input side, after pre-fusing RMSNorm gains) and W_down/W_out (output side). But **two sites are not absorbable in weight space**, for architectural (not engineering) reasons:
  - **The MLP gating nonlinearity:** "We first insert a Hadamard operation into the feed-forward network, before the down-projection matrix" — an **online** transform is *required* because the elementwise/gating nonlinearity regenerates structure no weight-space absorption removes.
  - **RoPE:** "**The existence of Pos prevents us from directly fusing the Hadamard matrix into W_q and W_k**"; QuaRot applies online **head-wise** Hadamards to queries and keys **after** RoPE (matching rotations ⇒ attention scores invariant), while **values take a separate path** (a head-wise rotation pre-fused into W_v, no online transform).
  **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2404.00456 HTML v2, all four sub-claims quoted at source). *Consequence:* these are hard obstructions H-DD inherits — a "rotate to a canonical basis then drop" pipeline cannot rotate freely across the MLP nonlinearity or through RoPE; any drop touching those must be validated end-to-end, not by matrix-local algebra.
- **F4 — Basis choice is a large free parameter (SpinQuant), and its R1–R4 taxonomy is RESOLVED.** Among output-invariant rotations, *which* one you pick swings downstream zero-shot reasoning by **up to 13 points** at 4-bit; SpinQuant therefore **learns** the rotations (Cayley/Stiefel-manifold optimisation). Draft flagged the **R1–R4 taxonomy UNVERIFIED**; I resolved it this session: **R1, R2 are offline rotations merged into weights** (learned); **R3, R4 are online Hadamards** at exactly the two non-absorbable sites — R3 for KV-cache/values, R4 inside the FFN before the down-projection. **[established] (13-pt swing, learned rotations) / [claimed] (R1–R4 detail, from authoritative secondary + OpenReview PDF, not the arXiv primary this session) [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2405.16406). *Note the convergence:* SpinQuant's online sites (R3/R4) coincide with QuaRot's non-absorbable sites — an independent corroboration that the KV/values path and the FFN-down path are the two structural obstructions. *Consequence:* "normalise to a canonical basis" must **pin the basis-selection rule as a registered IV**; it is not a free modelling choice.
- **F5 — The strict gauge of an UNMODIFIED RMSNorm donor is only signed permutations B_d (draft's §2.3 sharpening).** With generic per-channel RMSNorm gain the exact symmetry group of the coordinate system is **B_d = S_d ⋉ {±1}^d**, not O(d); LayerNorm charts have **S_d up to a global sign flip**. Permutation-only alignment is symmetry-*incomplete* (worked failure: SAE reconstruction NMSE **0.004 → 1.08** under permutation-only vs signed-permutation transport; a Qwen steering vector preserves 95.8% vs 17.2% of its effect). **[claimed] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2606.31963 — paper is REAL; single author, John Sweeney, Sideplane AI, June 2026; claims verified at source). **WEIGHT caveat (draft's, endorsed):** a very-recent single-author preprint scoped to *interpretability-artifact transport across checkpoints* — the sharpest available statement of the strict gauge, not the last word. *Risk direction is favourable:* if this B_d claim were wrong, the plausible error is the true gauge being *larger* (more freedom, e.g. O(d) available un-refactored), which would make an H-DD derivation built on B_d **conservative, not broken**. O(d) freedom appears legitimately only *after* the gain-fusing / LayerNorm-conversion surgery of F3. *Consequence:* P3-D-DD must derive **three layers** per donor — (a) strict gauge as-parameterised (B_d / S_d-with-sign-flip, re-derived not inherited), (b) enlarged group after declared refactorings, (c) per-block Q_ℓ schemes with explicit residual bridges — each validated with **end-to-end logits over a pinned corpus**.
- **F6 — A shared/rotated basis is *meaningful* but the "near-zero barrier" holds only at medium scale (LMC).** Jointly-learned functionality-preserving transformations align independently-trained transformers onto low-barrier linear-interpolation paths: **near-zero barriers for medium WikiText LMs** but only **small (nonzero) barriers at billion-parameter scale**; ViT-L keeps ≥69% ImageNet top-1 along the path. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2606.23607 — REAL; Li & Shen, MBZUAI; claims match, incl. the draft's correct restriction that "near-zero" is medium-scale only). The stronger "rotation hypothesis" (same function up to a one-batch-learnable residual rotation) remains **[UNVERIFIED-DIRECT]** — do not premise on it (draft agrees).
- **F7 — Fragility floor: one scalar can dominate (super weights).** Pruning as few as **one** scalar parameter can raise perplexity **3 orders of magnitude** and drop zero-shot to chance; super weights are identifiable **data-free in a single forward pass** and induce rare "super activations." **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2411.07191). *Consequence:* H-DD must locate and **document** donor super weights and treat their handling as a **pre-registered stratified/control variable** (protected vs not) — else a null could be a single-coordinate artifact and a positive could hinge on an unexamined protection.

### 1.2 The (iv) comparator — retrained structured pruning + KD best practice

- **F8 — Minitron is the strongest at-mechanism recipe; its win is ISO-COMPUTE with UNEQUAL tokens (verified).** Joint width (hidden/head/MLP, activation-importance) and/or depth pruning + KD retraining on **<3% of original data**; 8B+4B from a 15B donor at **up to 40× fewer training tokens per model**, **1.8× family compute saving**, **up to 16% MMLU over from-scratch**, comparable to Mistral-7B/Gemma-7B/Llama-3-8B. **Critically for the §3 accounting:** the from-scratch comparison is **explicitly iso-compute, not equal-token** — "approaches are compared under iso-compute settings," and the from-scratch twin receives *more tokens* because distillation pays the teacher's forward passes (reported ~**13.5% MMLU gain at equivalent compute**). **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2407.14679; 40×/1.8×/<3%/16% + iso-compute framing all confirmed). **Residual:** the draft's exact Table-11 figures (4B: 37.81 MMLU on 100B KD tokens vs 26.24 on 150B from-scratch tokens) are **[UNVERIFIED-DIRECT]** this session (HTML render 404'd) — but the load-bearing *direction* (iso-compute, KD-pays-teacher, unequal tokens) is confirmed independently, so the accounting argument stands even if the two decimals are author-attested.
- **F9 — Sheared-LLaMA is the other pillar; its "3%" excludes donor pretraining.** Targeted structured pruning to a pre-specified shape (layers/heads/intermediate/hidden) end-to-end + **dynamic batch loading** (re-mix by per-domain loss); LLaMA2-7B→1.3B/2.7B **beats Pythia/INCITE/OpenLLaMA/TinyLlama** at equal size with **~3% of from-scratch compute**. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2310.06694 — all three sub-claims verbatim). *Accounting caveat (load-bearing for §3):* the 3% **excludes the donor's pretraining compute** — honest as a *derived-artifact* claim, not as a *total-cost* claim.
- **F10 — Cheap-recovery and depth-slack anchors.** LLM-Pruner: gradient-based structural pruning over dependency-coupled groups + LoRA recovery in ~3h/50k samples at 20% (cheap-recovery lower bound) **[claimed] [memory]** (arXiv:2305.11627, not re-verified this session). Deeper-layers: layer pruning by representational similarity + QLoRA healing gives minimal QA loss until **~half the layers** are removed on some open models **[claimed] [memory]** (arXiv:2403.17887, not re-verified). *Consequence:* generic capacity slack exists in **depth**; a concept-aligned H-DD method must beat the **depth-pruned+healed** control too, or it has found generic slack, not semantics.
- **F11 — Distillation budgeting is a measured science, but regime-bounded.** A distillation scaling law predicts student performance from the compute split; distillation beats supervised pretraining **only below a compute level that scales with student size, and only when the teacher is free/amortised** (capacity gap = learning-capacity gap). **[claimed] [memory]** (arXiv:2502.08606, not re-verified this session). *Consequence:* whether R-1-scale (135M/160M) recovery falls in KD's winning regime is **not computed** — P3-D-DD must run the law's *actual calculation at donor scale* (and/or pilot both recovery modes) before pinning KD over plain continued pretraining. **[speculative]** hypothesis, not a result.
- **F12 — Small donors have LESS slack (extrapolation guard).** SliceGPT 25%-slice keeps ~99% zero-shot on LLAMA2-70B/OPT-66B but only **90% on Phi-2 (2.7B)**. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2401.15024). *Consequence:* the published big-model retention numbers must **never** be extrapolated down to R-1; the direction of the size-vs-retention curve is *against* easy wins exactly where H-DD is cheapest to test — which raises the value of the cheap P3-E-DD-0 kill.

### 1.3 SAE geometry + interp-guided pruning — a wall of measured negatives, one usable template

The rev-1 "SAE features give a removable orthogonal block" assumption is dead several times over; every kill is **fundamental** (not fixable by scaling the SAE).

- **F13 — Superposition is the baseline geometry.** Models store more sparse features than dimensions as **non-orthogonal** directions; polysemanticity is strategic compression with a phase transition. **[established] [memory/search]** (arXiv:2209.10652, foundational; draft's claim matches the canonical result). *There is no per-dimension concept ownership to excise.*
- **F14 — "Linear" concept directions are non-orthogonal in the naive basis; the right geometry is a causal (whitened) inner product.** Verified at source: the paper introduces a **non-Euclidean inner product** that "respects language structure," under which causally separable concepts behave orthogonally; validated on LLaMA-2; the "fundamental role of the choice of inner product" is explicit. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2311.03658). *Consequence:* any H-DD concept-subspace identification must **estimate the metric**, not assume Euclidean orthogonality.
- **F15 — SAE dictionaries are not canonical, not stable, not complete.** No canonical units (larger SAEs contain novel latents; latents decompose into meta-latents) **[claimed] [memory]** (2502.04878); ~30% feature overlap across seeds **[claimed] [memory]** (2501.16615); feature absorption across hundreds of SAEs, not fixable by size/sparsity **[claimed] [memory]** (2409.14507); at best quasi-orthogonal **[claimed] [memory]** (2503.24277). Not re-verified at source this session (draft verified 2502.04878/2501.16615/2409.14507 on 2026-07-11; 2503.24277 is a KB record). *All are fundamental limits, not instrument limits.*
- **F16 — Downstream SAE utility is currently negative vs baselines.** SAE probes do not consistently beat standard probes across data-scarcity/imbalance/noise/shift; GDM's mech-interp team found dense linear probes vastly outperform SAE probes (incl. OOD harmful-intent) and **deprioritised fundamental SAE research** (2025-03-26), while noting SAEs stay useful for dataset debugging. **[claimed] [memory]** (2502.16681; Alignment Forum GDM post — not re-verified at source this session). *Grounds the probe-first ordering.*
- **F17 — The one usable template: interp-guided causal ablation (SHIFT).** Sparse Feature Circuits discovers causal circuits of interpretable features by attribution; **SHIFT improves classifier generalisation by ablating human-judged task-irrelevant features**. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2403.19647 — both sub-claims confirmed at source). *This is the closest published ancestor of P3-E-DD-0 steps (1)–(2): identify → causally ablate → measure selective consequence.* SAE-guided **unlearning** also works (SSPU: −3.22% harmful-knowledge accuracy vs strongest baseline, utility retained) **[claimed] [memory]** (2505.24428) — but removes **without** backfilling.
- **F18 — Pruning × SAE geometry: geometry ≠ causal importance.** Under magnitude/Wanda pruning, RARE SAE features survive better than frequent ones (Spearman ρ=−1.0 in 11/17 conditions); Wanda preserves feature geometry up to 3.7× better than magnitude; **geometric preservation does NOT predict causal importance**. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2603.25325 — REAL, March 2026, three families Gemma-3-1B/Gemma-2-2B/Llama-3.2-1B; claims match). *Consequence:* "the concept direction survived the cut" is not evidence the capability survived — only intervention is.
- **F19 — The localisation threat (the single strongest published reason H-DD's "identify" step can fail even when it *looks* successful).** Causal-tracing localisation gives **NO insight into which MLP layer is best to edit**; editing succeeds across layers regardless of where tracing places the fact. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2301.04213, verbatim at source). *Consequence:* "concept found" carries **zero** weight until P3-E-DD-0's selective-ablation **and** store-reinjection-restoration criteria pass. Localisation evidence is inadmissible.

SmolLM2 (135M/360M/1.7B); the **1.7B trained ~11T tokens** but the abstract does **not** state the 135M/360M token counts — do not attribute ~11T to the 135M donor. **[claimed] [memory]** (2502.02737, not re-verified this session; the draft's caution is correct and preserved).
- **F21 — Architecture dominates below 1B (MobileLLM).** Deep-thin + embedding sharing + GQA gives +2.7pp/+4.3pp over prior 125M/350M SOTA. **[claimed] [memory]** (2402.14905). *Consequence:* a width-slashed H-DD artifact lands in an architecture regime where these choices dominate; controls (iii)/(iv) must share them by construction.
- **F22 — ~2 bits of factual knowledge per parameter (the capacity anchor).** Verified at source: LMs "can and only can store 2 bits of knowledge per parameter" over controlled synthetic tuple corpora, **stable to int8**; GPT-2-with-RoPE matches/surpasses LLaMA/Mistral at storage; MoE/junk-data/under-exposure reduce it. **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (arXiv:2404.05405). **USE LIMIT (load-bearing):** this is a **synthetic information-capacity sanity bound**, NOT an upper bound on the packed store bytes needed to backfill a parameter cut — an external store additionally pays addressing/keys/schema/retrieval-index overhead, so "drop N params ⇒ ≲2N store bits" is an **invalid inference**. Use only as a sanity check in P3-D-POWER's G1 analogue.

### 1.5 H-DD's closest prior art

- **F23 — No exact prior art located (session-bounded, must be re-run under a logged protocol).** No published work found that (i) identifies concept-associated parameters/subspaces in an **existing** pretrained model, (ii) removes them structurally, **and** (iii) restores the lost capability specifically from an **external symbolic/typed store**. **[speculative → search-bounded] [search 2026-07-19].** The draft's own **audit-trail limitation** is endorsed and elevated: the search strings/engines/dates were not logged, so this supports "no exact prior art located in this review," **not** a durable novelty finding — P3-D-DD (or coordinator) must re-run under a logged protocol before the novelty claim is load-bearing.
- **F24 — Nearest neighbours (each verified where load-bearing).**
  - **LMLM (closest to the destination).** Verified at HTML v2: **"LmLm stands for Large Memory Language Model"** (the draft's name correction is right — an earlier version read "Limited Memory"); the pretraining corpus is annotated with DB lookups and **retrieved factual spans are masked from the loss** so the model learns lookup over memorisation; facts editable/unlearnable by DB operations. **NEW magnitude resolved this session (draft had it UNVERIFIED):** **LmLm-382M ≈ LLaMA2-7B on factual-precision benchmarks.** **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (2505.15962). *But it trains a NEW model — no donor surgery, no identified subspace, flat fact-DB not a typed kernel.* H-DD's distinctive claim survives; LMLM is (a) evidence the store-backfilled *end state* is reachable and (b) a candidate R-1 comparator arm.
  - **Retrieval substitutes for parametric capacity (RETRO/RETROfit):** RETRO ≈ GPT-3/Jurassic-1 at **25× fewer params** given a 2T-token DB; RETROfit retrofits a *pretrained* transformer cheaply. **[claimed] [memory]** (2112.04426, not re-verified). *Retrofit = add-without-removal; nobody measured removal + retrieval restoration on the same model.*
  - **Factual capacity is separable + relocatable (Memory Layers at Scale):** sparse trainable KV memory replacing FFN capacity beats dense at >2× compute and MoE at matched resources, largest gains on **factual** tasks, scaled to 128B memory params. **[claimed] [memory]** (2412.09764). *The training-side statement of H-DD's premise.*

  - **Removal-without-backfill exists** (SSPU, F17) = steps (i)+(ii) without (iii).
  - **Against:** Hase (F19) says the "identified" step's evidence must be interventional; 2-bit/param (F22) says what's dropped is *capacity*, not neatly addressable facts. Both are why P3-E-DD-0 exists.

## 2. PROVEN-invariance table (component → what surgery preserves it → citation + verification status)

"Preserves" = the network **function** is unchanged (exact) OR the transform is the licit reparameterisation that *enables* a later lossy drop. **Every drop is on the lossy side of the line — invariance gets you a favourable basis; the deletion is always an approximation.**

| Component | Exactly invariant under… | Precondition / non-absorbable obstruction | Source | Verification (this session) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Inter-block normalisation | Residual-stream orthogonal Q, absorbed into neighbours | **Must be parameter-free RMSNorm**; RMSNorm gains pre-fused into adjacent weights first; **LayerNorm must be converted** (mean-subtract + scale absorbed) before any Q | SliceGPT 2401.15024; QuaRot 2404.00456 | **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (RMSNorm(X)=RMSNorm(XQᵀ)Q quoted at QuaRot HTML v2) |
| Input projections W_q,W_k,W_v,W_up,W_gate | Q absorbed as Qᵀ·diag(α)·W (after gain-fusing) | Only after RMSNorm gains pre-fused | QuaRot 2404.00456 | **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** |

| Embedding / unembedding | Rotated by the same global Q | Tied embeddings couple Q_first=Q_last (or need explicit de-tying); slicing errors of the two sides become coupled | SliceGPT 2401.15024; QuaRot 2404.00456 | **[established] (rotation) / [speculative] (tied-coupling derivation)** |
| MLP inner dimension (across gating nonlinearity) | **NOT weight-absorbable** — requires an **online Hadamard before W_down** | Elementwise/gating nonlinearity regenerates structure no weight-space absorption removes | QuaRot 2404.00456 (= SpinQuant R4) | **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** (quoted at source; corroborated by SpinQuant R4) |

| Attention value / KV-cache path | Head-wise rotation pre-fused into W_v (offline; no online transform) | Separate path from Q/K | QuaRot 2404.00456 (= SpinQuant R3) | **[established] [search 2026-07-19]** |

**Reading of the table for H-DD:** the only *exact* freedoms are (i) rotations of the residual stream under RMSNorm (or after conversion), fully specified per-site, with **two hard non-absorbable obstructions** (MLP gating, RoPE) that force online transforms, and (ii) the strict signed-permutation gauge of the untouched parameterisation. **None of these licenses a dimension drop.** The drop is always lossy against a proxy objective; its behavioural damage must be measured directly. A design that treats "we have proven invariances" as "we can delete concept-dimensions losslessly" has conflated the two sides of the line.

## 3. The concrete implication for P3-D-DD (the dimension-drop design)

1. **Invariance derivation = three layers per donor, all validated by end-to-end logits over a pinned corpus** (not per-matrix numerical checks alone): (a) strict gauge as-parameterised (expect B_d / S_d-with-sign-flip — **re-derive**, the source is a single-author preprint); (b) enlarged group after declared refactorings (gain-fusing, LayerNorm→RMSNorm conversion); (c) per-block Q_ℓ schemes **with** explicit Q_{ℓ−1}ᵀQ_ℓ residual bridges. Include the RoPE head-dim obstruction and the tied-embedding coupling (SmolLM2-135M ties input/output embeddings) as explicit numerical checks. Donor pair chosen precisely to exercise two different groups: SmolLM2-135M (RMSNorm, RoPE, tied) vs Pythia-160M (LayerNorm, parallel attn+MLP, untied).
2. **Basis-selection is a registered IV** — PCA-on-calibration (SliceGPT), learned rotation (SpinQuant; the 13-pt swing means this is not free), or probe-metric-aligned (causal inner product). One primary, pinned in the prereg.
3. **Identification-method ordering** (prior plausibility): (a) supervised probes in an estimated causal/whitened metric; (b) SAE-derived subspaces via pinned projection **with signed-permutation-correct transport** (F5 — permutation-only breaks); (c) dense attribution/ablation screening à la SHIFT. "Concept found" is inadmissible until P3-E-DD-0's selective-ablation + restoration pass (F19). Mapper-mediated matching inherits proxy precision ~0.71 (registry/verdicts/m0a-llmproxy.json).

## 4. The strongest reason H-DD might be FUNDAMENTALLY unsound (not merely capability-limited)

The task asks for this explicitly. Synthesised from the verified literature, the strongest fundamental threat is a **granularity-and-objective mismatch**, in three linked parts, each individually backed by a verified result:

1. **There is no dimension-indexed concept to excise (superposition + capacity).** F13 (superposition) says features live as **non-orthogonal directions in a code with more features than dimensions**; F22 (2-bit/param) says what a parameter stores is dense, distributed, information-theoretic capacity. So H-DD's premise that "~80% of dimensions correspond to encoded concepts" that can be *individually* identified and dropped is in **direct tension with the measured geometry**. You can identify a concept-associated *direction* (a subspace), but the drop operates on *dimensions/parameters*; dropping dimensions removes capacity spread across **all** superposed features, not a clean concept. The identification granularity (directions) and the excision granularity (coordinates) are mismatched by the geometry itself — a fundamental, not engineering, mismatch. This is why F14 forces a whitened metric even to *talk* about concept orthogonality, and why F18 shows geometric survival ≠ causal survival.

2. **The backfill is proven only for facts, but the drop removes computation.** The verified prior art (F24) shows an external store substitutes for **memorised facts** — RETRO, LMLM, Memory Layers all report their gains on **factual** tasks, and LMLM explicitly masks *factual spans* from the loss. But dense parameters in superposition do more than store facts; they perform the reasoning/composition/in-context computation that superposition packs in. **No verified work shows a symbolic/typed external store can restore distributed dense *computation***, only fact lookup. If what H-DD drops is capacity-for-computation rather than a fact-table, the store cannot back it up **in kind** — the one thing evidenced (fact relocation) is not the thing needed (computation relocation).

3. **Even correct localisation does not predict manipulability (Hase, F19).** This is the empirically-demonstrated dissociation that closes the loop: suppose H-DD *does* correctly identify where a concept is represented. F19 shows that location tells you **nothing** about whether removing/editing there yields the intended selective change. So the "identify" step, even if it succeeds by every representational metric, provides no warrant for the "drop + backfill" step.

Together: H-DD's chain (identify concept → drop its dimensions → restore from store) rests on **three empirically-contested correspondences**, each threatened by a verified result — the concept↔coordinate correspondence (superposition), the dropped-capacity↔store correspondence (fact-only backfill), and the localisation↔editability correspondence (Hase). The honest statement is: **these are geometry-level, not instrument-level, threats — but none is a proof of impossibility.** Each is "the strongest published reason to doubt," which is exactly why the design's cheap causal gate (P3-E-DD-0) demands **interventional** evidence — selective ablation **and** store-reinjection restoration — rather than any representational/localisation evidence. The design is correctly armoured against the threats it faces; the threats are real and fundamental-flavoured; the falsifier is cheap and correctly targeted. If P3-E-DD-0 fails, H-DD is killed before any pruning run — which is the right cost profile for a bet this aggressive (still the programme's boldest extrapolation, ASM-0804).
