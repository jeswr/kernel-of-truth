## 0. The one-paragraph verdict for this line

Four decades of hooking external structure into neural LMs sort into a strikingly consistent pattern. **What worked**: external knowledge delivered (i) as *text in context* (RAG — the outright winner), (ii) as *values retrieved by the model's own keys* (kNN-LM, Memorizing Transformers, RETRO), or (iii) as *dense vectors through a trained adapter into an already-trained model* (E-BERT, xRAG, KBLaM, GraphToken, ToolkenGPT) — always lossy, always mediated by learned components. **What failed or stalled**: welding external *fixed semantic coordinates* into the model as load-bearing structure (the KEPLM line, dead at BERT scale with probes showing only marginal knowledge actually integrated), and forcing models to *natively read and write a frozen semantic vector space* (LCM/SONAR — worse scaling exponent; the 2025 successor CALM recovers the concept-level efficiency win precisely by *abandoning* the fixed semantic space for a learned non-semantic autoencoder latent). The SAE literature, corrected hard in 2025 (feature absorption, seed non-identifiability, non-canonicity, GDM deprioritisation), simultaneously *undermines* "the model will adopt our concepts internally" and *creates* the kernel's best opening: learned feature dictionaries demonstrably lack exactly the stability, labels, and versioning the kernel provides externally (E8/A6). The neuro-symbolic record supplies the one rigorous template where model capability *demonstrably changed an outcome*: a strong neural model as **author/translator into a formal language whose deterministic engine owns correctness** (AlphaGeometry, Logic-LM). Every verified capability win in the whole survey is on the **symbol side** (authoring, translating, reconciling); there is **no verified case of model capability fixing the consumer side** — making a neural network treat an imposed vector as its meaning.

## 1. Frozen / fixed / non-trained embeddings in transformers — the record, extended

The falsification stream's chain stands after re-verification and is not re-argued here: input embeddings are already static at inference; models converge around frozen random or glyph-derived embeddings (at a 5–10× convergence cost for badly matched ones); the input table is among the least performance-critical parameter blocks; semantic initialisation of new tokens is a head start that washes out; the model's actual concept knowledge lives in trained mid-layer MLPs (ROME); early layers re-represent ("detokenize") whatever vectors they are given (fixed-vectors-in-llms.md §§1, 5, 6) **[established]**.

What Line 3 adds — the *injection-mechanics* family that has emerged around frozen **LLMs** (the dual of frozen embeddings), because it defines the achievable interface topology:

- **Soft prompts** (Lester et al., EMNLP 2021, arXiv:2104.08691) **[established]**: a few trained continuous input vectors steer a frozen LLM to match full fine-tuning at scale. High-bandwidth input channel; vectors are *learned against the specific model* and non-transferable (Su et al., NAACL 2022).
- **ToolkenGPT** (Hao et al., NeurIPS 2023, arXiv:2305.11554) **[established]**: tools added to a *frozen* LLM as new vocabulary items ("toolkens") by training **only their embeddings**. Works well for calling dozens–hundreds of tools. This is the exact inverse of the kernel: model frozen, new-symbol embedding *trained*. It demonstrates that a frozen transformer's input space has room for new functional symbols — provided the symbol's vector is optimised *into* that space.
- **GraphToken / "Let Your Graph Do the Talking"** (Perozzi et al., 2024, arXiv:2402.05862) **[claimed]**: a GNN encodes graph structure into soft tokens for a frozen LLM; large gains (up to ~73% relative) on graph-reasoning benchmarks versus text-serialised graphs. The closest thing to "GNN-style concept I/O into an LLM" that exists — and the encoder is **trained end-to-end against the frozen host**. Structured input helps; *canonical* structured input has never been tried.
- **xRAG** (arXiv:2405.13792, NeurIPS 2024) and **KBLaM** (arXiv:2410.10450, ICLR 2025) **[established]**: documents/KB triples as dense vectors through trained projectors; efficient and real, with quantified loss (xRAG retains ~62–73% of the capability of giving the model the text). No 2025–2026 successor found that removes the trained bridge; the follow-on literature (e.g. decoupled-MoE parametric injection, arXiv:2606.14243) continues to *train* the injection pathway **[established as absence]**.

**§1 verdict.** The interface topology is now well mapped: input-side injection into transformers works **iff** something trained mediates — either the injected vector was optimised into the host's input distribution (ToolkenGPT, soft prompts), or a trained encoder/projector produces it (GraphToken, xRAG, KBLaM), or a trained linear aligner maps it (E-BERT). The cell "hand-set, never-trained vectors, zero trained interface" remains empty of positive results **[established as absence, re-verified 2026-07-08]**. For the kernel this fixes the *only* defensible injection design: A2 (canonical vectors + small learned per-model adapter), exactly as the falsification stream concluded.

## 2. Knowledge injection (ERNIE-THU, KnowBERT, K-BERT, K-Adapter, KEPLER, plug-in adapters) — verdict layer

The catalogue and numbers are in `nsm-and-knowledge-injection.md` §§4–5 and are re-affirmed. The verdict layer this report owns:

1. **The gains were real but task-local** (entity typing, relation extraction — tasks nearly read off the injected KG), with GLUE parity, not gains, self-reported by ERNIE and KEPLER **[established]**.
2. **Little knowledge actually entered.** Hou, Fu & Sachan (arXiv:2202.00964) probe-measured "only a marginal amount of knowledge is successfully integrated" into ERNIE/K-Adapter, and scaling the integration corpus did not help **[established]**. The consumer-side machinery — fusion layers under a likelihood objective — re-encodes and largely discards the imposed structure. This is the same mechanism as the frozen-embedding "arbitrary identifier" result, observed from the other side.
3. **The line died of scaling + retrieval, not of bad inputs.** No GPT-3-class embedding-injection system ever demonstrated an advantage; LLM-era surveys (arXiv:2306.11489, TKDE 2024) reposition KGs as curriculum/retrieval/evaluation aids **[established]**.
4. **Capability lens (owned here, argued in §7):** better entity linking and better KG quality — the steps a stronger model unblocks — were *not* the binding constraint. The binding constraint was that (a) the consumer re-encodes injected structure, and (b) a stronger host model needs the injection less: the knowledge the KEPLMs injected is precisely what scaling learned anyway, and what retrieval serves better for the long tail (Mallen et al., ACL 2023). **A Fable-class model does not resurrect this line; it is what killed it.** Verdict: **fundamental as formulated — and capability-*obviated*, not capability-limited** **[established facts, our inference]**.

## 3. Retrieval and external memory: kNN-LM, REALM, RETRO, Memorizing Transformers — the interface-locality law

This is the *successful* branch of "hook external representations into an LM", and its design regularities are the strongest empirical constraints on any kernel injection plan. New territory for the report set.

- **kNN-LM** (Khandelwal et al., ICLR 2020, arXiv:1911.00172) **[established]**: interpolate the LM's softmax with a nearest-neighbour lookup over a datastore of (hidden-state → next-token) pairs. WikiText-103 perplexity 18.65 → **15.79**, then-SOTA, **with no additional training**. The decisive detail: the datastore *keys are the model's own trained hidden states*. The external memory works because it is indexed in the model's native representation space.
- **REALM** (Guu et al., ICML 2020, arXiv:2002.08909) **[established]**: retrieval-augmented MLM pretraining with a jointly *learned* dense retriever; ~330M-parameter model reached ~40 EM on NaturalQuestions-Open, beating much larger closed-book models (including T5-11B) at the time. Knowledge externalised as **text**, retriever trained end-to-end.
- **RETRO** (Borgeaud et al., DeepMind, ICML 2022, arXiv:2112.04426) **[established]**: chunked cross-attention over neighbours retrieved from a 2-trillion-token database using **frozen BERT** chunk embeddings; a 7.5B RETRO is comparable on the Pile to models with ~25× more parameters (GPT-3/Jurassic-1 class). Two kernel-relevant details: (i) the retrieval representation *can* be frozen and generic — because what is retrieved and fed to the model is ultimately **text**, re-read by the trained decoder; the frozen vectors only *address* the memory, they never *carry the meaning into* the model; (ii) **RETROfit**: a pretrained LM can be retrofitted by training only the chunked-cross-attention (<10% of weights) — again, a small trained bridge is the price of admission.
- **InstructRetro / RETRO 48B** (Wang et al., NVIDIA, ICML 2024, arXiv:2310.07713) **[established]**: the largest retrieval-pretrained LM (43B GPT continued-pretrained on 100B tokens with retrieval from 1.2T). The startling ablation: after instruction tuning, **the retrieval encoder can be removed entirely** — the decoder alone matches the full architecture on QA. The benefit of retrieval-augmented pretraining had *migrated into the decoder's weights*. External apparatus, even when it works, tends to be **scaffolding the trained model absorbs and discards** — a deflationary precedent for any claim that an injected external structure will remain load-bearing.
- **Memorizing Transformers** (Wu et al., ICLR 2022, arXiv:2203.08913) **[established]**: kNN attention into a non-differentiable memory of the model's **own past key-value pairs**, up to 262K tokens; matches vanilla models with ~5× more parameters **[claimed at that ratio]**; a model pretrained *without* memory can be fine-tuned to use one. Again: the memory's contents are the model's own activations — no alien coordinate system.
- **Atlas** (Izacard et al., JMLR 2023, arXiv:2208.03299) **[established]**: retrieval-augmented few-shot learner; 11B model beats 540B PaLM on some knowledge tasks — the mature statement of "externalise knowledge as retrievable text, spend parameters on skill".

**The interface-locality law (the section's synthesis, ours) [established pattern → speculative as a law].** Across every working external-memory system, the representation that crosses the boundary into the model is one of exactly three things: (1) **text**, re-encoded by the model itself (REALM, RETRO, Atlas, RAG); (2) **the model's own activations** (kNN-LM keys and values, Memorizing Transformers); or (3) **vectors passed through a trained projector/adapter** (xRAG, KBLaM, E-BERT, GraphToken). No working system feeds a *foreign fixed coordinate system* raw. And where the external structure works best (InstructRetro), its benefit is absorbed into the weights and the apparatus becomes removable. Any kernel injection experiment that ignores this law is testing a cell the literature says is empty; any that respects it is in A2/A5 territory by construction.

## 4. Concept-level, sentence-level, and tokenizer-free models — LCM/SONAR after the dust, plus the 2025 turn

The falsification stream's LCM/SONAR/BLT analysis is re-affirmed (fixed-vectors-in-llms.md §4): LCM's real win was zero-shot multilinguality inherited from SONAR; the frozen SONAR space is fragile beyond ~200-char sentences and sparse as a generation target; concept-space variants scale worse than token LMs (SONAR-LLM's measured exponents: token α≈0.79 vs 0.49–0.57 for concept-space variants); no scaled LCM-2 has appeared as of 2026-07 **[established]**.

What Line 3 adds:

- **ByT5** (Xue et al., TACL 2022, arXiv:2105.13626) **[established]**: byte-level T5 matches/beats mT5 at matched parameter counts, is markedly more robust to noise and better on spelling/pronunciation-sensitive tasks, at real inference cost from longer sequences. **MEGABYTE** (arXiv:2305.07185) **[established]** makes byte-level competitive via fixed-size patches + local/global decomposition. **BLT** (arXiv:2412.09871) **[established]** closes the gap at 8B scale with *entropy-driven dynamic* patches. The through-line: the successful replacements for the tokenizer make the latent unit **smaller, learned, dynamic, and content-agnostic** — every step moves *away* from fixed semantic units, not toward them.
- **CALM — Continuous Autoregressive Language Models** (Shao et al., 2025, arXiv:2510.27688) **[claimed — recent, not yet independently replicated]**: next-**vector** prediction where the vector is a **learned high-fidelity autoencoder latent compressing K tokens** (not a semantic sentence space), with a likelihood-free training/evaluation toolkit. Reports a superior performance-compute frontier versus strong token baselines by increasing "semantic bandwidth per step". This is the LCM idea *rescued by removing exactly the property the kernel cares about*: the latent is engineered for reconstruction fidelity and modelability, not for canonical meaning. Read together, LCM → SONAR-LLM → CALM is a controlled natural experiment: concept-level *step compression* is good; a **frozen semantic coordinate space as the prediction target** is the part that hurt **[established trajectory; attribution speculative but strongly indicated]**.
- **Coconut** (arXiv:2412.06769) and latent-CoT faithfulness concerns (arXiv:2512.21711) **[claimed]**: unchanged from the falsification stream; latent concept-computation trades away inspectability.

**§4 verdict.** Dense concept-level I/O is alive as an *efficiency* programme (CALM) and dead so far as a *fixed-semantic-space* programme (LCM). The measured failure was never "sentences are the wrong unit"; it was "a frozen, externally fixed semantic geometry is a bad prediction target for gradient-trained models" — the same lesson as §§1–2 at a different altitude. A stronger authoring model does not change this: the scaling penalty is a property of the objective and target-space geometry, not of the content quality **[our inference from established results]**.

## 5. Sparse autoencoders and dictionary learning — the 2025 correction, and what it does to E8

The kernel's A6/E8 bet ("kernel as canonical, versioned, labeled coordinate system for learned features") depends on an accurate read of this fast-moving literature. State of play:

**The build-up (2023–24) [established]:** Cunningham et al. (arXiv:2309.08600, ICLR 2024) and Bricken et al. ("Towards Monosemanticity", Anthropic 2023) showed SAEs decompose LM activations into sparse, substantially interpretable features; Templeton et al. ("Scaling Monosemanticity", 2024) scaled to millions of features on Claude 3 Sonnet with causal steering demonstrations (Golden Gate Claude); OpenAI scaled to 16M latents on GPT-4 (arXiv:2406.04093). Superposition (arXiv:2209.10652) remains the operative regime: concepts in trained LMs are many-per-dimension distributed directions, not slots.

**The correction (2024–2026) — each item verified this week:**
- **Feature absorption** (Chanin et al., arXiv:2409.14507; NeurIPS 2025 oral) **[established]**: sparsity optimisation makes seemingly monosemantic latents fail to fire on hierarchical subsets ("starts with S" absorbed into "short"); varying size/sparsity does not fix it. Learned dictionaries systematically mangle **hierarchy** — the single structure the kernel encodes best.
- **Seed non-identifiability** (Paulo & Belrose, arXiv:2501.16615) **[established]**: SAEs trained on the same model and data with different seeds learn substantially different dictionaries — only ~30% of features shared across seeds for a 131K-latent TopK SAE on Llama 3 8B. There is no canonical learned dictionary to align to; there are *samples from a distribution of dictionaries*.
- **Non-canonicity** (Leask et al., arXiv:2502.04878, ICLR 2025) **[established]**: SAE stitching shows dictionaries are incomplete; meta-SAEs show larger-SAE latents decompose into smaller-SAE latents ("Einstein" → "scientist" + "Germany" + "famous person") — SAE units are neither atomic nor unique.
- **Downstream-task failure and deprioritisation** (Google DeepMind mech-interp team, March 2025 progress update; "Negative Results for SAEs on Downstream Tasks") **[established]**: SAE features failed to beat simple linear-probe baselines on OOD concept detection and steering at scale; GDM explicitly deprioritised SAE research. Companion position: "Use SAEs to Discover Unknown Concepts, Not to Act on Known Concepts" (arXiv:2506.23845) **[claimed]**.
- **Cross-model universality — the door that stayed open**: SAE feature spaces across different LLMs show measurable similarity (arXiv:2410.06981; arXiv:2410.06672), and **Universal SAEs** (Thasarathan et al., arXiv:2502.03714, ICML 2025) train one shared dictionary across multiple models, recovering aligned concepts **[claimed, growing]**. Convergence is again real *up to learned machinery* — same shape as vec2vec/relative-representations in the falsification stream.
- **Auto-interpretation** (Bills et al. 2023; EleutherAI, arXiv:2410.13928) **[established]**: labeling features is done by LLMs, at LLM quality; label quality tracks the strength of the explainer model — a genuine capability-limited component inside the SAE pipeline.
- **Adjacent precedent to flag for novelty claims**: the **Hyperdimensional Probe** (arXiv:2509.25045) **[claimed]** already uses VSA hypervector codebooks to decode LLM residual-stream representations across several open models, and Knittel et al. 2024 interpret GPT-2 internals as VSA-like circuits. Neither builds canonical *content-addressed* concept vectors, but "VSA meets LLM internals" is no longer virgin territory; E8's novelty claim must be phrased against these.

**§5 verdict — for E8, double-edged and net positive [our synthesis].** The correction *helps* the kernel's instrument pitch: the specific defects now documented (seed instability, absorption of hierarchy, non-atomicity, no labels, no versioning) are exactly the properties the kernel supplies externally and deterministically — a canonical, versioned label/coordinate space for feature dictionaries answers a documented need that per-model learned dictionaries provably cannot self-supply. It also *raises the bar*: (i) if only ~30% of features are seed-stable, E8's alignment target must be the *stable subset* or cross-seed feature clusters, not one SAE's dictionary, and its nulls must include cross-seed correspondence without the kernel; (ii) GDM's negative results establish the acceptance test any interp instrument now faces — **beat the simple baseline on a downstream task** (probing/steering/regression detection), or be decoration; (iii) whether learned feature geometry aligns with a *designed* geometry at all remains completely open — the universality results align learned spaces with each other, never with a constructed one. E8 tests an empty cell, which is the good kind of risk.

## 6. Neuro-symbolic integration — the one place capability demonstrably changed the outcome

- **Taxonomy and surveys [established]:** Kautz's six-type taxonomy (AAAI 2020 address; AI Magazine 2022) organises the space; Garcez & Lamb, "Neurosymbolic AI: the 3rd Wave" (Artif. Intell. Review 2023) states the programme; the sober empirical check is Hamilton et al., "Is Neuro-Symbolic AI Meeting its Promise in NLP?" (arXiv:2202.12205; Semantic Web Journal): through ~2022, hybrid NLP systems showed promise concentrated in narrow domains, with brittleness at the neural↔symbolic interface — the translation step — recurring as the failure point.
- **The autoformalization turn (2023–2025) [established]:** Logic-LM (Pan et al., Findings of EMNLP 2023, arXiv:2305.12295): LLM translates NL problems into formal specs, a deterministic solver infers, solver error messages drive LLM self-refinement — +39.2% over standard prompting, +18.4% over CoT across five logical-reasoning datasets. LINC (Olausson et al., EMNLP 2023) replicates the pattern with FOL provers. "Intermediate Languages Matter" (arXiv:2502.17216) **[claimed]**: the *choice of formal language* materially moves neurosymbolic LLM performance — the formalism is a first-class experimental variable.
- **AlphaGeometry** (Trinh et al., *Nature* 2024) and **AlphaProof** (DeepMind 2024; IMO silver) **[established]**: near-gold olympiad geometry and IMO-medal proof search from a neural model *authoring constructions/tactics* inside a deterministic symbolic engine (DD+AR; Lean) that **owns correctness**, trained on ~100M *synthetically authored* proofs. This is the strongest existing demonstration that the historical neuro-symbolic bottleneck was the symbol-side authoring/translation capability, now removed.

**§6 verdict.** The neuro-symbolic record splits exactly along the seam the maintainer's lens predicts. Failures through ~2022 concentrated at NL→formal translation and formal-content authoring — **capability-limited, now measurably unblocked** (Logic-LM's numbers; AlphaGeometry's medals). What did *not* change: in every success the symbolic engine is **outside** the network, deterministic, and authoritative, coupled through a formal language; nobody has made the network *internally* symbolic. The winning template — **neural author + deterministic external verifier/engine** — maps onto the kernel as: LLM authors explications; the kernel validates (closed grammar, fail-closed), canonicalises (JCS/SHA-256), and encodes (deterministic vectors); correctness properties live on the kernel side. That is the A5/A6 shape, not the A1 shape **[our synthesis]**.

### 7.1 The three laws the table obeys (the hard-skeptic reading)

**Law 1 — Capability wins live on the symbol side, without exception.** Every confirmed capability-limited failure (rows 1–6) is a failure to **author, translate, label, or reconcile symbolic content**. Every confirmed fundamental failure (rows 10–19) is on the **consumer side**: how a gradient-trained network receives, stores, re-encodes, or scales with imposed structure. Across ~60 systems surveyed by the three lines, there is **no verified instance of model capability fixing a consumer-side failure**. The programme's "why now" argument is therefore legitimate *only* in its authoring form (the kernel can now be *built and populated*), and illegitimate in any form that implies stronger models will *absorb* canonical vectors as meaning **[our synthesis; each input fact established]**.

**Law 2 — The capability gradient is double-edged, and the second edge cuts the kernel.** The same capability that authors explications also: (a) reads them perfectly well **as text** — and in-context text beats every parametric injection method measured (Cohen et al. ripple results; RAG's victory over KEPLMs; InstructRetro's removable encoder); (b) learns long-tail knowledge better, shrinking the gap injection targeted; (c) re-encodes arbitrary frozen symbols faster (row 12). So as models strengthen, the *text interface* to the kernel improves automatically, while the *vector interface* keeps paying its capability-independent tolls (trained adapter, ~62–73% fidelity ceiling à la xRAG, distribution-matching constraints). The null hypothesis any injection experiment must beat is not "no kernel" — it is **"the same kernel content, rendered as text in context"**. If kernel vectors cannot beat their own textual rendering, the vector-injection tier has no value-add at any capability level **[our synthesis]**.

**Law 3 — Where capability genuinely changed outcomes, the symbolic system was outside the network and owned correctness.** AlphaGeometry, AlphaProof, Logic-LM, and (in microcosm) our own encoder+validator loop share one topology: neural author ↔ formal language ↔ deterministic engine that verifies/computes, with error signals flowing back to the author. Prior failures in the same domains trace to the pre-LLM absence of the author, not to the engine. The kernel fits this template *as the engine/verifier artifact* (identity, validation, decode-and-verify, drift detection: A5/A6), and violates it as an injectee (A1). The strongest capability-based case for the kernel is thus the one that requires no injection at all **[our synthesis]**.

### 7.2 Direct answer to the maintainer's question

Which prior failures would a Fable-class model reverse? **Confidently:** the neuro-symbolic translation bottleneck (already reversed, with measured numbers); the authoring famine that stalled VSA, NSM, Schank-style decomposition, DL-embedding canonicalisation, and conceptual-space instantiation — these lines stopped where hand-authoring stopped, and a Fable-class author/validator loop removes that stop. **Not at all:** the KEPLM collapse (capability killed it), the LCM scaling penalty, the arbitrary-identifier result, superposition, editing ripple effects, geometric expressiveness limits, content-addressability of trained representations, Fodor, Harnad, and the 65-atom insufficiency. The convenient story — "knowledge injection failed because 2019 models were weak" — is **specifically false**: the injection literature's own probes show the failure was in what fusion-under-likelihood does to imposed structure, and every capability increase since has widened, not narrowed, the gap between text-interface and vector-interface knowledge delivery.

## 8. Implications for the decisive experiments

1. **Add the text-rendering null arm (Law 2) to E1/E4.** Pre-register: kernel content as Minimal-English text in context vs kernel content as injected vectors vs shuffled-kernel. If vectors ≤ text at matched token/FLOP budget, the injection tier is dead independent of the content-vs-content-free question. This is the cheapest decisive control the current design lacks.
2. **Respect the interface-locality law in any injection design (§3).** The only supported topologies: kernel vectors through a small learned adapter into a trained host (E-BERT protocol; A2), or kernel *values* addressed by model-native keys (kNN-LM/KBLaM shape). Raw kernel coordinates as native model I/O (A1) tests a cell every adjacent literature says is empty — keep it only as the cheap falsification it is budgeted to be, and treat a null as expected, not disappointing.
3. **Recalibrate E8 against the 2025 SAE record (§5).** Target the *seed-stable* feature subset or cross-seed clusters (Paulo–Belrose: ~30% shared), include cross-seed/cross-model correspondence *without* the kernel as the null, use absorption-aware hierarchical probes (the kernel's explicit hierarchy vs SAEs' documented hierarchy-mangling is the sharpest differential prediction), and adopt GDM's acceptance test: the instrument must beat a simple baseline on a downstream task (e.g., cross-version semantic regression detection), not merely show alignment above chance. Cite and differentiate from the Hyperdimensional Probe (arXiv:2509.25045) in any writeup.
4. **Adopt the Logic-LM/AlphaGeometry template for the authoring loop (§6).** Feed the kernel validator's fail-closed error codes back to the authoring model as structured refinement signals — the measured +18–39% pattern; and treat the choice of explication formalism as an experimental variable ("Intermediate Languages Matter"), which the AST profile system already supports.
5. **Expect absorption, not adoption, from any successful injection.** InstructRetro's removable encoder and the vocabulary-expansion wash-out results predict that even a *successful* kernel injection migrates into host weights, leaving the kernel apparatus removable. Design E7-class claims about *training efficiency* (did the kernel speed learning?) rather than *permanent residence* (is the kernel still the model's representation?) — the latter is unmeasurable and, per rows 12–13, almost certainly false.
6. **Position the programme's strong-capability claim precisely.** The defensible sentence is: *"Model capability has, for the first time, made canonical symbolic content authorable, validatable, and maintainable at scale; it has simultaneously made injecting that content into models as fixed vectors less necessary, not more."* Both halves are load-bearing; the prospectus should carry both.

## 9. Open questions

- Does *any* alignment exist between designed (kernel) geometry and learned feature geometry beyond what shuffled-kernel nulls produce? (E8's empty cell — nothing in the convergence or USAE literature touches designed spaces.)
- Is there a scale at which frozen semantic structure stops costing convergence — or does the 5–10× penalty for mismatched frozen embeddings grow with model scale? (No published scaling study of frozen-embedding penalties; would materially inform E7.)
- Can the CALM latent lane be hybridised — a learned autoencoder latent *regularised toward* kernel coordinates — without inheriting the LCM scaling penalty? (Untested; the one architectural middle path between rows 7 and 11.)
- Does the text-rendered kernel (Minimal English explications in context) measurably reduce hallucination/drift on kernel-covered concepts versus no kernel — the injection-free value test that none of the surveyed literature answers?
- How far does the ToolkenGPT precedent stretch: can ~10³ concept "toolkens" with *frozen kernel-derived* embeddings (only a shared adapter trained) match per-token learned embeddings? (The minimal A2 experiment; direct, cheap, unrun.)
