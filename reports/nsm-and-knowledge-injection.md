# Computational NSM, definition-grounded vectors, and knowledge injection into LMs

**Kernel of Truth programme — literature survey (web research), 2026-07-07.**
Scope: prior art bearing on the hypothesis that NSM-style structured explications over ~65 semantic primes can carry canonical, training-free vector representations forming a "kernel" inside LLM/LCM architectures. Coordination: sparq-org/sparq#1683.

Every substantive claim is tagged **[established]** (replicated / uncontroversial in the literature), **[claimed]** (asserted by its authors, not independently verified), or **[speculative]** (inference, ours or others').

## HALF A — Computational NSM and definition-grounded semantics

### 1. NSM computationally

#### 1.1 DeepNSM (Baiden, Kirby & Jones-style OSU work; arXiv 2505.11764, 2025)

The first systematic application of LLMs to NSM explication generation, with the first automatic evaluation metrics for explications ([arXiv:2505.11764](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.11764), code at OSU-STARLAB/DeepNSM).

**Task and data [established as description of the paper]:**
- Dataset built from WordNet: 88,078 word senses filtered to ~44k word–example–explication entries (43k train / 1k val / 149 hand-curated test). Candidate explications (~440k) were generated by Gemini-2.0-Flash at temperature 0.7 and filtered by the paper's own metrics (score ≥ 35 threshold; <15% of candidates survived).
- Fine-tuned models: Llama-3.2-1B and Llama-3.1-8B (LoRA r=64, 4-bit).

**Metrics [claimed — the metrics are the paper's own invention]:**
- *Legality*: ratio of semantic primes to total words (penalises non-prime "molecules"); −10..+10.
- *Substitutability*: log-probability improvement when the explication is substituted for the target word in ambiguous passages, plus minimality and entailment sub-tests, averaged across 7–12B grader LLMs; capped at 40.
- *Overall explication score* (≤100); circular explications (containing the target word) score 0.
- *Cross-translatability*: round-trip translation through low-resource languages (e.g. Kinyarwanda), measured by BLEU and BERT-embedding similarity.

**Results [claimed]:**
| System | Explication score | Legality | Substitutability | Kinyarwanda round-trip BLEU |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dictionary definitions | 13.4 | −4.7 | — | 35.1 |
| Llama-3.2-1B base | 6.1 | — | — | — |
| GPT-4o | 22.9 | 4.6 | 6.95 | 51.3 |
| DeepNSM-1B | 23.2 | 5.1 | 7.02 | 56.9 |
| DeepNSM-8B | 24.6 | 5.4 | 7.34 | 55.6 |

Human qualitative ranking: DeepNSM top-ranked 46% vs GPT-4o 28% [claimed]. Fine-tuning gave +30.9% prime usage and −22.3% molecule usage vs Llama baselines [claimed].

**Honest reading for the kernel:**
- Quality ceiling is low in absolute terms: 24.6/100 on the paper's own composite. Legality ~5/10 means explications still lean heavily on non-prime vocabulary. **Automatic explications are nowhere near NSM-practitioner quality** [established — the authors themselves say outputs "should not be considered fully accurate representations of word meanings" and require human verification].
- The evaluation is LLM-circular: substitutability is judged by other LLMs, and the training data was generated and filtered by the same metric family. There is no gold human-authored explication corpus at scale (NSM practitioners have published only hundreds of explications over 50 years) [established].
- The paper does not enforce NSM grammar (no constrained decoding to primes, no grammatical constraints on prime combinations) — flagged by the authors as future work [established]. This is exactly the gap the kernel's explication grammar targets.

#### 1.2 Earlier machine-readable NSM

- **Zamblera (2010), "Computational NSM: a Prolog-based notation"** — a PROLOG notation and parser-generator for NSM texts; the DeepNSM related-work section cites it as the prior PROLOG encoding [established as existing; no evaluation record found]. Note: the PROLOG encoding is Zamblera's; **Avery Andrews' contribution is different** — his *Reconciling NSM and Formal Semantics* (ANU, 2014–2016, [semanticsarchive](https://semanticsarchive.net/Archive/2YxMmIwN/NSM_Formal_Semantics_v4.pdf), Australian J. Linguistics 2016) argues NSM explications can be given formal (Montague-style) interpretations, and uses Prolog's "defined but not eliminable" predicates as the model for how primes like BECAUSE function. It is a formalisation argument, not a working system [established].
- **Rodgers et al.** — word vectors aligned with semantic primes for the ACT-R cognitive architecture (cited in DeepNSM related work). Directly relevant prior art for "prime-grounded vectors"; small-scale, no NLP benchmark record found [established as existing].
- **Fähndrich et al. (2014; PhD TU Berlin 2018)** — semantic decomposition into NSM primes to build semantic graphs for marker-passing algorithms ([Faehndrich 2014](http://www.xn--fhndrich-0za.de/res/Faehndrich2014.pdf)); decomposition bottoms out in primes via recursive dictionary/WordNet lookups. Demonstrated on similarity/WSD-style tasks; never adopted more broadly [established as existing; utility [claimed]].
- **Minimal English (Goddard ed. 2018, *Minimal English for a Global World*, Palgrave; [Springer](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-319-62512-6))** — a practically usable superset of NSM: primes plus vetted cross-translatable "molecules" and relaxed grammar, aimed at non-specialists (diplomacy, health, law, science communication) [established]. **Design lesson for the kernel: even the NSM school concedes that pure primes are unusable at scale; a workable explication language needs a staged molecule vocabulary.** Minimal English is human-facing prose, not machine-readable — no formal grammar or tooling ships with it [established].

#### 1.3 The strongest published criticisms (the kernel inherits these)

- **Riemer 2006, "Reductive Paraphrase and Meaning: A Critique of Wierzbickian Semantics" (*Linguistics & Philosophy* 29; [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10988-006-0001-4))** [established as the leading critique]: (i) the *substitutability* criterion — an explication is validated if it can replace the word in context — is circular, because judging substitution adequacy already requires knowing the meaning being explicated; (ii) the reductive requirement (definiens simpler than definiendum) is not consistently achievable — NSM explications smuggle in complexity via prime *combinations* and "canonical contexts" that do disambiguation work the theory doesn't account for; (iii) NSM's syntactic evidence for polysemy decisions is applied inconsistently. Riemer (2016) generalises: decompositional methods have "considerable heuristic utility" but face serious problems *as theories of underlying semantic structure*. **Kernel translation: substitutability-based validation of explications (which DeepNSM also uses) is the weakest link; treat explications as useful canonical descriptions, not as discovered mental structure.**
- **von Fintel & Matthewson 2008, "Universals in Semantics" (*The Linguistic Review* 25; [PDF](https://web.mit.edu/fintel/fintel-matthewson-2008-universals.pdf))** [established]: surveying cross-linguistic evidence, they find "remarkably few convincing semantic universals" at the lexical level, reject the claim that primes are intuitively self-explanatory simple words, and stress that formal semantics offers explicit truth-conditional accounts where NSM offers paraphrase. Their considered view: the prime inventory's empirical status is unproven, not refuted.
- **Bohnemeyer** [established]: fieldwork on Yucatec Maya (1998 dissertation; 2002 *The Grammar of Time Reference in Yukatek Maya*; 2003 "NSM without the Strong Lexicalization Hypothesis," *Theoretical Linguistics*) shows Yucatec **lacks lexical exponents of the putative primes BEFORE and AFTER** — no adverbs, connectives or tenses encoding ordering of arbitrary events; temporal order is conveyed pragmatically and via more specific expressions ('yesterday', 'formerly'). His proposed repair — drop the Strong Lexicalization Hypothesis (every prime lexicalised in every language) — concedes the empirical universality claim while keeping the metalanguage. The NSM camp's reply (Goddard & Wierzbicka, e.g. "Semantic fieldwork and lexical universals," *Studies in Language* 38, 2014) disputes the fieldwork's interpretation [established as an ongoing dispute].
- Additional recurring critiques [established]: explications' English-biased scaffolding despite universality claims (translation-studies critiques, e.g. "Turning the tide," 2015); vagueness of prime identity across allolexes; and the observation that NSM has produced few explications that other lexicographers adopt.

**Net for the kernel:** NSM's *engineering* value (a small closed vocabulary, cross-translatable, with a constrained grammar) survives these critiques; its *scientific* claims (innate universal conceptual primitives; explications = mental representations) do not stand unchallenged. A kernel built on NSM should claim the former, not the latter [speculative — our reading, but consistent with Riemer 2016 and Bohnemeyer's weakened-NSM position].

### 2. Definition-grounded vector semantics

Do vectors built from *definitions* (not corpus co-occurrence) capture meaning?

- **Hill, Cho, Korhonen & Bengio 2016, "Learning to Understand Phrases by Embedding the Dictionary" (TACL; [ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/Q16-1002/))** [established]: RNN/BOW models trained to map dictionary definitions to the defined word's embedding solve reverse-dictionary lookup and crossword QA **as well as or better than commercial systems** (OneLook). First strong demonstration that definitions alone carry enough signal to locate words in embedding space.
- **Noraset et al. 2017, "Definition Modeling" (AAAI; [paper](https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/AAAI/article/view/10996))** [established]: the inverse task — generate a definition from a word embedding. Works as a probe of embedding content; generation quality was modest (RNN-era BLEU), and later work (SemEval-2022 CODWOE task paper, [arXiv:2205.13858](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2205.13858)) found **BLEU misleading for definition quality** [established].
- **dict2vec (Tissier et al., EMNLP 2017; [ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/D17-1024/))** [established]: augments word2vec training with strong/weak word pairs extracted from dictionary entries; consistent improvements on 11 word-similarity datasets, small gains on text classification. Definitions as *supplement* to co-occurrence.
- **CPAE (Bosc & Vincent, EMNLP 2018; [ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/D18-1181/))** [established]: consistency-penalised autoencoder over definitions — embeddings trained **exclusively on dictionary data** beat distributional baselines on semantic *similarity* (SimLex/SimVerb-style) benchmarks; best variant still needs pretrained-vector initialisation, and later comparisons found its edge benchmark-dependent [established]. This is the cleanest evidence that definition-only training captures similarity (as opposed to relatedness) unusually well.
- **DefSent (Tsukagoshi et al., ACL 2021; [arXiv:2105.04339](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2105.04339)) and DefSent+ (2024; [arXiv:2405.16153](https://arxiv.org/html/2405.16153v2))** [established]: fine-tuning sentence encoders to predict a word from its definition sentence yields competitive unsupervised STS performance — definition sentences are a strong, cheap supervision signal for sentence embeddings.
- **SemEval-2022 Task 1 CODWOE ([task paper](https://aclanthology.org/2022.semeval-1.1.pdf))** [established]: bidirectional conversion between glosses and embeddings across 5 languages. Reverse-dictionary direction (gloss → vector) works reasonably; definition-generation direction lags, and metric validity is a live problem. I.e., **definitions → vectors is the tractable direction; vectors → definitions is not yet reliable.**
- **Multi-relational hyperbolic definition embeddings (Valentino et al.; [arXiv:2305.07303](https://arxiv.org/html/2305.07303))** [claimed]: embeddings built purely from definitions in hyperbolic space organise a definitional hierarchy and remain competitive on similarity — evidence the *structure* of definitions (genus/differentia) is exploitable geometrically.
- **CPAE-adjacent caveat, benchmarks generally**: word-similarity benchmarks themselves are criticised — **Faruqui et al. 2016, "Problems With Evaluation of Word Embeddings Using Word Similarity Tasks" ([arXiv:1605.02276](https://arxiv.org/abs/1605.02276))** found word-similarity scores correlate poorly with downstream task performance [established]. So "definition vectors win on SimLex" is real but weaker evidence than it looks.

**Verdict:** definitions demonstrably carry enough signal to (a) locate words in a vector space (reverse dictionary), (b) beat co-occurrence on *similarity* (vs relatedness) tests, and (c) supervise sentence encoders. But every strong result either initialises from, or is evaluated against, distributionally trained spaces; **no published system builds a competitive semantic space from definitions with no distributional component at all** [established, as absence of evidence in the surveyed literature].

### 3. Graph/ontology-grounded word vectors

- **Retrofitting (Faruqui et al., NAACL 2015; [ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/N15-1184/))** [established]: post-hoc graph regularisation pulling linked words (WordNet, FrameNet, PPDB) together. Cheap, embedding-agnostic, consistent gains on lexical-semantic benchmarks across languages; became a standard baseline.
- **Counter-fitting (Mrkšić et al., NAACL 2016; [arXiv:1603.00892](https://arxiv.org/abs/1603.00892))** [established]: injecting synonymy *and antonymy* constraints; SimLex-999 state of the art at the time (ρ = 0.74); and — the rare genuine downstream result in this line — robust improvements in **dialogue state tracking** across domains.
- **ConceptNet Numberbatch (Speer et al.; [SemEval-2017 Task 2 paper, arXiv:1704.03560](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1704.03560))** [established]: ensemble of word2vec + GloVe (+ OpenSubtitles) fused with the ConceptNet graph via *expanded retrofitting*. **First place in both subtasks of SemEval-2017 Task 2 (multilingual & cross-lingual word similarity), and first in each of English, German, Italian, Spanish.** The high-water mark for structure-injected static vectors. Note the recipe: graph structure *refines* distributional vectors; the graph alone does not produce them.
- **Poincaré embeddings (Nickel & Kiela, NeurIPS 2017; [paper](https://papers.nips.cc/paper/7213-poincare-embeddings-for-learning-hierarchical-representations))** [established]: WordNet noun hypernymy (82,114 nouns, 743,241 edges) embeds in hyperbolic space with dramatically fewer dimensions than Euclidean — e.g. strong reconstruction/link-prediction at 5–10 dims where Euclidean needs hundreds. Shows *taxonomic* structure has a natural low-dimensional geometry — relevant to how much capacity a structured kernel actually needs.
- **path2vec (Kutuzov et al., \*SEM 2019; [arXiv:1808.05611](https://arxiv.org/abs/1808.05611))** [established]: embeddings trained to approximate WordNet graph distances; competitive on similarity/WSD vs graph-embedding baselines, orders of magnitude faster than on-graph computation.

**Where it stops helping [established, pattern across the literature]:**
1. Gains concentrate on **intrinsic lexical benchmarks** (similarity, hierarchy reconstruction); downstream transfer is thin (counter-fitting's DST win is the notable exception), and the benchmarks themselves correlate poorly with downstream utility (Faruqui et al. 2016).
2. Injecting *similarity/synonymy/antonymy* structure helps; injecting deeper relational structure yields diminishing returns — retrofitting's one-hop graph regularisation was never usefully extended to compositional/definitional structure at benchmark-moving scale.
3. The entire static-vector line was **superseded by contextual embeddings (ELMo/BERT, 2018–19)**: contextual models captured most of what retrofitting added, and the retrofitting literature effectively ended. Numberbatch survives today mainly as a lightweight resource, not a competitive representation.

## HALF B — Knowledge injection into transformer LMs

### 4. Entity/KG-enhanced LMs: the catalogue and the honest track record

**The catalogue [established as descriptions]:**
- **ERNIE (Tsinghua; Zhang et al., ACL 2019; [arXiv:1905.07129](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.07129))**: injects TransE entity embeddings via a fusion "K-Encoder". Key detail for the kernel: *"The entity embeddings are fixed during training"* — **frozen external concept vectors, with only the fusion layers learned**. Gains: Open Entity typing 75.56 vs BERT 73.56 F1 (+2.0); FewRel +3.4; TACRED +2.0; **GLUE: parity with BERT-base** (better CoLA/RTE, worse STS-B/MRPC).
- **ERNIE (Baidu; Sun et al. 2019 → ERNIE 3.0, [arXiv:2107.02137](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2107.02137))**: no KG vectors — entity/phrase-level *masking*; knowledge enters as a pretraining curriculum. ERNIE 3.0 posted strong Chinese-benchmark and SuperGLUE results [claimed by Baidu; benchmarks since surpassed by plain LLMs].
- **KnowBERT (Peters et al., EMNLP 2019)**: entity linkers + attention over WordNet/Wikipedia entity embeddings, recontextualisation. Gains on relation extraction, entity typing, WSD; adds inference cost.
- **K-BERT (Liu et al., AAAI 2020)**: splices KG triples into the input sentence tree with soft-position embeddings and a visibility matrix; gains mostly on **domain-specific** Chinese tasks (medicine, finance), marginal on open-domain [established].
- **LUKE (Yamada et al., EMNLP 2020; [ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/2020.emnlp-main.523/))**: RoBERTa + a **500k-entity vocabulary** with its own embeddings and entity-aware self-attention; then-SOTA on SQuAD 1.1, CoNLL-2003 NER, ReCoRD, TACRED, Open Entity [established]. The strongest *real* result in this family — but on entity-centric tasks, and the entity embeddings are trained, not imported.
- **K-Adapter (Wang et al., 2021; [arXiv:2002.01808](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2002.01808))**: frozen RoBERTa + plug-in adapters trained on factual/linguistic objectives; modest gains on entity typing/QA/relation classification.
- **KEPLER (Wang et al., TACL 2021; [arXiv:1911.06136](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1911.06136))**: joint MLM + knowledge-embedding objective over Wikidata5M entity *descriptions* (entities encoded from their text descriptions — nearest published cousin of "concept = embedding of its definition"). Gains on relation extraction/entity typing; **GLUE: comparable to RoBERTa** — i.e. no general-language benefit [established].
- **DRAGON (Yasunaga et al., NeurIPS 2022; [paper](https://papers.nips.cc/paper_files/paper/2022/hash/f224f056694bcfe465c5d84579785761-Abstract-Conference.html))**: joint text+KG-subgraph pretraining (MLM + link prediction); genuine gains over GreaseLM/QAGNN on knowledge- and reasoning-intensive QA (CommonsenseQA, OpenBookQA, MedQA), strongest in low-resource and multi-step settings [claimed, widely cited]. Still BERT-scale.

**The honest track record:**
- **Gains were real but narrow** [established]: improvements concentrate on entity typing, relation extraction, entity-centric QA — tasks whose labels are nearly read off the injected KG. On general language understanding (GLUE), ERNIE and KEPLER report *parity*, not gains, with their own base models.
- **Direct measurement says little knowledge actually enters the LM** [established as a published finding]: Hou, Fu & Sachan 2022, "What Has Been Enhanced in my Knowledge-Enhanced Language Model?" ([arXiv:2202.00964](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.00964)), using a graph-convolution probe, found **"only a marginal amount of knowledge is successfully integrated"** into ERNIE and K-Adapter, that the two integrate *different* relation types, and that scaling the knowledge-integration corpus does not help.
- **The gains evaporated as plain LMs scaled** [established as the field's trajectory]: the KEPLM line effectively stops at BERT/RoBERTa scale (2019–2022). No GPT-3-class model with ERNIE/KnowBERT-style embedding injection ever demonstrated an advantage; surveys written in the LLM era ("Give Us the Facts," Yang et al., IEEE TKDE 2024; [arXiv:2306.11489](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.11489); also the KEPLM survey [arXiv:2110.00269](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2110.00269)) concede LLMs outperform KEPLMs broadly and reposition KGs as (i) training-signal/curriculum, (ii) retrieval sources (RAG/GraphRAG), and (iii) evaluation/grounding aids — not as embedding-injection targets.
- **The durable residual case is the long tail** [established]: Mallen et al., ACL 2023 ([arXiv:2212.10511](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.10511)) — scaling fails to improve memorisation of long-tail facts, and retrieval-augmented small models beat much larger plain LMs on tail entities; Kandpal et al., ICML 2023 ([PMLR](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v202/kandpal23a/kandpal23a.pdf)) — fact recall tracks pretraining co-occurrence counts. But note: the field's remedy for the tail became **retrieval**, not parametric injection.
- **Critical-eye surveys**: Hu et al. ([arXiv:2110.00269](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2110.00269)) catalogue the family and note static, context-insensitive knowledge and noise interference as systematic weaknesses; "Give Us the Facts" (TKDE 2024) is the closest to an honest post-mortem [established].

### 5. Knowledge tokens / entity vocabularies (the kernel's insertion mechanics)

Approaches that add entities/concepts as first-class vocabulary items:

- **LUKE**: 500k entities as real tokens with their own embedding matrix, trained jointly with MLM over hyperlinked Wikipedia. Embeddings are *learned distributionally* (from mention contexts), not built from structure [established].
- **ERNIE (THU)**: entity vectors pretrained by TransE on Wikidata and **frozen** during LM pretraining ("The entity embeddings are fixed during training") — the LM learns only the fusion/alignment machinery. It worked (see §4 numbers). **Direct precedent for frozen, externally-constructed concept vectors coexisting with a trained LM** [established].
- **E-BERT (Poerner, Waltinger & Schütze, Findings of EMNLP 2020; [ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/2020.findings-emnlp.71/))** — the strongest precedent for the kernel's mechanics [established]: Wikipedia2Vec entity vectors are aligned into BERT's *wordpiece embedding space* with a simple **linear transformation, with no change to the encoder and no further pretraining**, and fed to BERT as if they were wordpieces. Result: new state of the art on LAMA (unsupervised fact probing), beating BERT, ERNIE and KnowBERT, plus gains on relation classification and entity linking. **Lesson: a frozen transformer will accept externally-built vectors as input tokens if and only if they are linearly aligned to its input distribution; the concept vector itself can be built elsewhere.** Caveat: Wikipedia2Vec vectors are themselves distributional (trained on Wikipedia co-occurrence + link graph), not definitional/canonical.
- **Entities as Experts (Févry et al., EMNLP 2020; [arXiv:2004.07202](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.07202))**: a sparse external *entity memory* accessed mid-transformer; learned from text with entity-linking supervision; matched a 10× larger generator on TriviaQA [claimed]. **Facts as Experts ([arXiv:2007.00849](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2007.00849))** adds a triple memory built compositionally from entity embeddings — supporting the idea that structured objects (triples) can be *composed from* concept vectors and remain usable by the LM [claimed].
- **Wikipedia2Vec (Yamada et al.)**: the standard source of standalone entity vectors for several of the above [established].

**Pattern across the family [established]:** frozen external vectors were tried (ERNIE, E-BERT, K-Adapter's frozen backbone) and they work *as interfaces* — but in every successful case (a) the vectors were distributionally trained, and (b) a learned alignment/fusion layer mediates. No published system uses *canonical, structure-derived, training-free* vectors as the entity vocabulary. That slot in the design space is genuinely unoccupied [established as absence in the surveyed literature — the closest is Rodgers et al.'s prime-aligned vectors for ACT-R, §1.2].

### 6. Knowledge editing as the contrast case — and what interpretability says about "a concept = one reserved vector"

**The editing programme [established]:** ROME (Meng et al., NeurIPS 2022) models mid-layer MLPs as linear key–value memories and rewrites one fact with a rank-one update, locating facts by causal tracing; MEMIT scales this to thousands of edits across several layers.

**What the editing literature actually reveals:**
- **Localisation is illusory** [established]: Hase et al., NeurIPS 2023, "Does Localization Inform Editing?" ([arXiv:2301.04213](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.04213)) — causal-tracing localisation does *not* predict where editing works; facts can be overridden by editing layers other than where tracing says they live. Storage is distributed and redundant across layers.
- **Edits don't propagate** [established]: Cohen et al., TACL 2024, "Ripple Effects of Knowledge Editing" ([arXiv:2307.12976](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2307.12976)) — after editing one fact, logically entailed facts update with only 38–66% accuracy; simple in-context injection beats ROME by 10–29 points. The "fact" was never a discrete stored object; it is entangled with its inferential neighbourhood.
- **Editing at scale degrades the model** [established]: Gupta et al. 2024 ([arXiv:2401.07453](https://arxiv.org/html/2401.07453v3)) — sequential ROME/MEMIT edits cause gradual then catastrophic forgetting; and "Model Editing Harms General Abilities of LLMs" (Gu et al. 2024) shows downstream degradation (QA, sentiment, reasoning, summarisation) after editing.
- **Superposition/polysemanticity** [established in toy models; strongly supported in real LMs]: Elhage et al. 2022, "Toy Models of Superposition" ([arXiv:2209.10652](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.10652)) — networks store many more sparse features than they have dimensions by superposing them on non-orthogonal directions; sparsity governs a phase transition between mono- and polysemantic neurons. Sparse-autoencoder work (Bricken et al. 2023 "Towards Monosemanticity"; Templeton et al. 2024 "Scaling Monosemanticity", Anthropic) recovers millions of interpretable features from real models, each a *direction spread across many neurons*, confirming superposition as the operative regime in production LMs [established as the consensus interpretability reading].
- **Partial support for concept-as-vector** [claimed]: the linear representation hypothesis (Park et al., NeurIPS 2023 workshop / ICML 2024; [arXiv:2311.03658](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.03658)) — high-level concepts do behave as linear directions usable for probing *and* steering, with a causal inner product unifying the two. And cross-model SAE feature-space similarity ([arXiv:2410.06981](https://arxiv.org/html/2410.06981v1)) plus the Platonic Representation Hypothesis (Huh et al. 2024) suggest independently trained models converge toward similar feature geometries [claimed].

**Implication for the kernel, stated honestly:**
1. Concepts in trained LMs are **directions, not slots** — distributed, superposed, non-orthogonal, and redundantly stored across layers. Nothing in the editing or interpretability literature supports the existence (or stability) of "one reserved vector per concept" *inside* a trained network's residual stream; superposition actively predicts the model will overload any reserved subspace with other features unless the architecture *enforces* the reservation (e.g., frozen embedding rows, orthogonality penalties, or a protected subspace) [established → speculative at the design end].
2. However, the kernel hypothesis as an **interface** claim — canonical frozen vectors at the input/output vocabulary boundary, aligned to the model by a learned linear map — has working precedent (E-BERT, ERNIE) and is *not* contradicted by superposition, which concerns internal representations [established precedent; kernel extension speculative].
3. The editing literature is, if anything, an argument *for* the kernel's motivation: mutable-weight storage of facts is fragile, non-local, and damages the model when modified. Built-in, content-addressed, unlearnable concept anchors are the natural contrast design. But the same literature warns that the *inferential consequences* of a concept (its ripple neighbourhood) live in the weights regardless — a frozen anchor vector does not by itself make the model *use* the concept correctly [established observations; the inference is ours, speculative].

## Closing verdicts

**(a) Strongest evidence that definition/structure-grounded vectors carry real semantic signal [established]:**
ConceptNet Numberbatch winning *all* subtasks and *all four* languages of SemEval-2017 Task 2 by fusing graph structure into distributional vectors; Hill et al. 2016's definition-trained models matching commercial reverse-dictionary systems; CPAE beating distributional baselines on similarity while trained (almost) purely on dictionaries; DefSent showing definition sentences alone competitively supervise sentence encoders; Poincaré embeddings showing taxonomic structure fits in strikingly few dimensions. Consistent caveat: every headline win is on intrinsic/lexical benchmarks whose downstream correlation is known to be weak (Faruqui et al. 2016), and every strong system keeps a distributional component.

**(b) Strongest evidence that knowledge injection into LMs stopped paying off [established]:**
Hou & Sachan's probe finding only *marginal* knowledge actually integrated into ERNIE/K-Adapter; GLUE parity (not gains) reported by ERNIE and KEPLER themselves; the abrupt end of the KEPLM line at BERT scale with no LLM-scale successor; LLM-era surveys (TKDE 2024) repositioning KGs from injection targets to retrieval/curriculum aids; and Mallen et al./Kandpal et al. showing the one durable gap (long-tail facts) is better served by retrieval than by parametric injection. Ripple-effect results add that even successful parametric insertion of a fact fails to update its entailments (38–66% accuracy), while trivial in-context provision beats ROME by 10–29 points.

**(c) What superposition implies for the kernel hypothesis [honest statement]:**
Superposition (Elhage et al. 2022; SAE work 2023–24) says trained transformers represent concepts as many-per-dimension, distributed, non-orthogonal directions — so a *reserved internal vector per concept* contradicts how gradient descent uses capacity, and any unenforced reservation should be expected to erode into superposition during training. The linear representation hypothesis and cross-model feature convergence give the kernel its best interpretability support: concepts *are* vector-like directions, and different models may converge on similar geometries — but those directions are learned, model-specific in basis, and discovered post hoc, not canonical or training-free. The defensible kernel design that survives this literature: canonical concept vectors as a **frozen input/output vocabulary with a learned linear adapter** (E-BERT/ERNIE precedent), with the explicit expectation that internal processing re-encodes them into the model's own superposed basis; the claim that the kernel's vectors remain the model's *internal* representation of the concept is unsupported and likely false without architectural enforcement.
