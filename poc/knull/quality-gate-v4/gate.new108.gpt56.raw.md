## Part 1 — Defect audit

### 1. Register or word-choice faults

- **2:** “**possessed of life**” is an affected substitute for *alive* or *living*; “**the vital functions still going on**” then shifts awkwardly into colloquial phrasing.
- **5:** “**recoverable at need**” is conspicuously archaic diction for a modern computing sense; *recoverable when needed* is idiomatic.
- **13:** “**leaves of writing or print**” is a faulty collocation. Leaves may *bear* writing or print; they are not naturally “of writing or print.”
- **19:** “**soft-footed**” is a literary and imprecise substitute for the relevant anatomical description, such as *having padded paws*.
- **21:** “**a glad occasion**” is affected, antiquated diction in this context.
- **30:** “**under grief, pain, or other strong emotion**” uses the wrong collocation. One weeps *from* or *because of* grief or pain, not “under” it.
- **37:** “**above all one of some note**” is a strained use of *above all*. The required expression is *especially one of some importance*.
- **41:** “**the flaming state of what burns**” is an unnatural and conceptually muddled circumlocution for combustion or burning.
- **42:** “**an animal of the water**” is unidiomatic in place of *an aquatic animal*; “**gilled for breathing and finned for swimming**” is mannered, artificially symmetrical diction.
- **46:** “**apart from kin or romance**” compresses unlike categories into an unnatural construction. Friendship is not properly defined by being “apart from” kinship or romance.
- **50:** “**mourning over a loss**” substitutes the response to grief for grief itself. *Grief* is deep sorrow; *mourning* is its expression or observance.
- **59:** “**the arms being clasped about another person in affection**” is stiffly contrived, and “**in affection**” is the wrong collocation; *as an expression of affection* would be natural.
- **63:** “**to voice mirth or amusement**” is a strained collocation. One *expresses* mirth or *gives voice to* amusement.
- **82:** “**set down on a flat surface**” is an affected and inaccurate substitute for *represented*, *drawn*, *painted*, or *recorded*. Many pictures are not literally “set down.”
- **95:** “**that recurring state**” is an oddly deictic opening with no antecedent and sounds like literary exposition rather than a clean definition.

### 4. Non-definitional material

- **62:** “**so that it remains of that class for as long as it exists**” is an appended metaphysical claim about the permanence of classification, not part of the meaning of *kind*. It is also not generally true: things can change kind or be reclassified.

## Part 2 — Overall naturalness

**4/10.**

The collection is intelligible, and some entries are entirely natural—especially **1**, “**filled with fear or apprehension**,” which is the most natural entry. Others that read cleanly include 34, “**a domesticated carnivorous mammal of the wolf family**,” and 91, “**to move on foot at speed, both feet leaving the ground in each stride**.”

As a set, however, the definitions sound systematically manufactured in an old-fashioned pseudo-lexicographic voice. Telltale formulations include “**possessed of life**” (2), “**a glad occasion**” (21), “**above all one of some note**” (37), “**the flaming state of what burns**” (41), “**gilled for breathing and finned for swimming**” (42), and “**to voice mirth**” (63). The recurrent balanced clauses and literary circumlocutions make the entries feel rewritten to avoid ordinary dictionary wording.

There are also conspicuous precision problems that professional editing would catch: 23 restricts a child to someone “**below the age of puberty**”; 44 includes “**whatever is eaten or drunk**” as food; 75 equates a mother specifically with “**the woman who has borne a child**”; and 106 calls the water of “**seas**” both “**clear**” and “**tasteless**.”

The least natural entry is **62**: “**the class to which a thing belongs by its very nature, so that it remains of that class for as long as it exists**.” It reads as improvised philosophical commentary, not as a professionally edited definition.
