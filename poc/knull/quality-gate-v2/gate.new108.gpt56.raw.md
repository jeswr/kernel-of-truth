## Part 1 — Defect audit

### 1. Register or word-choice faults

- **5:** “**restored to circulation**” — an archived bookmark or record is restored to an active list, not normally “to circulation.”
- **8:** “**enter upon the first part of an action or process**” — a strained circumlocution for *begin*; one may “enter upon” an undertaking, but not naturally “enter upon its first part.”
- **13:** “**whether narrative, instructive, or of reference**” — faulty coordination and unidiomatic wording. The natural term is *a reference work*, not a composition “of reference.”
- **18:** “**enclosing a driver with a small number of passengers**” — an unnatural collocation. A car *accommodates* or *carries* a driver and passengers.
- **25:** “**cloth cut and sewn to the figure**” — cloth or clothing is cut and sewn *to fit the figure*, not “sewn to the figure.”
- **27:** “**surfaces answering to the light they reflect**” — “answering to” is misapplied; color depends on, corresponds to, or results from reflected light.
- **39:** “**begotten by him**” — needlessly archaic and conspicuously out of register in a modern general-dictionary definition.
- **51:** “**the water beside it**” — a strained and inaccurate counterpart to “the ground”; water is not generically situated “beside” the ground.
- **59:** “**a garment fitting tight about the body**” — overly informal and awkward here; edited prose would use *fitting tightly* or *fitting closely*.
- **62:** “**which it cannot cease to instance**” — *instance* is an affected and questionable substitute for *instantiate* or *be an example of*.
- **62:** “**the class that settles what counts as one such thing**” — “the class settles” is forced personification and unsuitable diction for a general-dictionary entry.
- **71:** “**the artificer**” — an affected, archaic substitute for *maker* in what is ostensibly a definition of that ordinary word.
- **77:** “**notes felt as expressive or beautiful**” — “felt as” is an unnatural collocation here; *perceived*, *experienced*, or *regarded as* would be idiomatic.
- **78:** “**at only a short distance away**” — a redundant blend of *at a short distance* and *a short distance away*.
- **86:** “**a fact ready to be produced when wanted**” — facts are recalled, stated, or brought to mind; “produced” is strained in this context.
- **88:** “**decay befell it**” — decay occurs, sets in, or affects something; it does not naturally “befall” it.

### 2. Vague anaphoric renaming

none found.

The occurrences of *thing*, *object*, and *creature* generally belong to the actual definiens or denote a different participant; they are not unnatural renamings of the headword referent.

### 3. Staged observers

none found.

Phrases such as “**perceptible by the eye**” in 104 directly define visibility and do not invent an onlooker or narrate an observer’s reaction.

### 4. Non-definitional material

- **10:** “**found in nearly every region of the earth, from the sparrow to the eagle**” — distributional information followed by a decorative sweep through examples, not part of the meaning of *bird*.
- **21:** “**with which such an occasion is distinguished from ordinary days**” — a literary reflection appended after the gathering or ceremony has already been defined.
- **30:** “**the weeping may be silent or audible, momentary or prolonged**” — narration of possible manifestations rather than meaning.
- **30:** “**in an infant it is the chief means of signalling distress**” — an account of crying’s practical function.
- **34:** “**the breeds differ widely in size and form**” — supplementary zoological information, not part of what *dog* means.
- **49:** “**the commonest herbage of the countryside, cropped by grazing animals**” — picturesque and partly functional commentary after *grass* has already been identified.
- **52:** “**the chief bodily instrument of human skill**” — an encomiastic flourish, not a necessary statement of the meaning of *hand*.
- **71:** “**to whose work an object owes its existence**” — decorative restatement after “the person or agent by whom a thing was fashioned or produced.”
- **83:** “**who is thereby entitled to expect its performance**” — a narrated normative consequence of receiving a promise.
- **85:** “**the shade conventionally used for warnings and for the signal that commands a stop**” — cultural-use information, not the meaning of the color itself.
- **94:** “**across which clouds pass and birds fly**” — picturesque scene-setting appended to the definition of *sky*.
- **96:** “**the gentler counterpart of the laugh**” — subjective literary commentary rather than a semantic criterion for *smile*.
- **101:** “**many kinds live to a great age**” — incidental biological information about trees.
- **101:** “**oaks, pines, and palms are familiar examples**” — an elementary illustrative appendage rather than definition proper.

## Part 2 — Overall naturalness

**4/10.**

The entries are usually intelligible and often factually serviceable, but as a collection they do not resemble professionally copy-edited modern dictionary prose. They are uniformly expansive, heavily semicolon-driven, and prone to pseudo-lexicographical ornament: “**begotten by him**” (39), “**the chief bodily instrument of human skill**” (52), “**the artificer to whose work an object owes its existence**” (71), and “**injury or decay befell it**” (88). The recurrent explanatory tails—“**distinguished from ordinary days**” (21), “**across which clouds pass and birds fly**” (94), and “**many kinds live to a great age**” (101)—make the set sound generated or deliberately antiquarian rather than edited for a present-day general dictionary.

The **least natural entry is 62**. Its “**category to which a thing belongs by its very nature**,” “**cannot cease to instance**,” and “**class that settles what counts as one such thing and as the same thing met before**” form a dense metaphysical excursus, not a usable general-dictionary definition of *kind*.

The **most natural entry is 32**: “**(of a person, animal, or plant) no longer alive; having permanently ceased the functions of life**.” It is concise, grammatically clean, appropriately neutral, and contains no ornamental tail.
