## Still-open or newly identified issues

### G0 read-time defense accepts unreasoned and double-retirement supersession

SEVERITY: freeze-blocking

Concrete mechanism: G0 validates the list shape and target arm but never validates `reason` or detects that a target was already superseded ([l3a_parse.py:283](</home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/analysis/l3a_parse.py:283>), [a5_nl.py:271](</home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/analysis/a5_nl.py:271>)). Verdict generation does not re-run append validation on a chain-verified log.

Why it matters: a schema-valid manually constructed chain with a missing reason or double-retirement can produce `instrument_valid=True` and a nominal PASS, contradicting the registered independent defense-in-depth contract.

### Invalid boolean totals can crash instead of returning INSTRUMENT-INVALID

SEVERITY: freeze-blocking

Concrete mechanism: G1 rejects the boolean, but the scorer continues to compute Wilson/Holm statistics using the malformed denominator. `n_control=True` crashes both scripts; a5 `n_covered=True` produces unserializable complex values.

Why it matters: the pre-registration promises malformed counts are classified `INSTRUMENT-INVALID`. These fixtures instead abort analysis with `ERR_P2_ANALYSIS`.

### Freeze scanner still treats superseded ASM-0425 as a live citation

Because the design still names ASM-0425 historically, both dry-runs reported pause flags for ASM-0425 despite the records replacing it.

Why it matters: freezing succeeds, but the mechanical output contradicts the remediation claim that no live citation reaches the stale entry and emits a misleading backlog signal.
