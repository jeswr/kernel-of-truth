## Still-open and new defects

### G0’s registered supersede path cannot exist in `kot-log/1`

SEVERITY: freeze-blocking

Concrete mechanism: Both scorers consume a top-level `supersedes` list ([l3](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/analysis/l3a_parse.py:228), [a5](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/analysis/a5_nl.py:217)), but `kot-log/1` has `additionalProperties:false` and defines no such property ([schema](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/registry/schema/kot-log-1.json:6)). The official `event:"supersede"` mechanism cannot substitute for it: `log-append.py` permits superseding only a row whose exit is not `ok` ([log append](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/tools/registry/log-append.py:147)), while only `exit:"ok"` rows reach the scorer ([verdict selector](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/tools/registry/verdict-gen.py:281)).

The raw G0 implementation also fails to type-check the field or constrain targets to the same arm. I constructed a dictionary-valued `supersedes` and a case where `answer-all` superseded a stale mapper row; both produced `g0_one_row_per_arm=True` and `instrument_valid=True`. Self/cycles are not explicitly detected, although because the row hash includes its own `supersedes` field, constructing one requires a SHA fixed point or collision.

Why it matters: The order-dependent verdict selection is fixed, but the promised successful-retry recovery path exists only in unit-test dictionaries and cannot enter the real hash-chained log. Freezing would pin an unexecutable protocol; successful duplicate rows can only poison the experiment permanently.

### The pinned NLB instrument emits an unloggable run body

SEVERITY: freeze-blocking

Concrete mechanism: `one_arm_body` emits a top-level `"arm"` in addition to `config.arm` ([instrument](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/tools/experiments/nlb/nlb_instrument.py:431)); `kot-log/1` rejects that undeclared property. It also omits `event`, `exit`, and `prereg_hash`; `log-append.py` stamps several envelope fields but does not add those and then requires them for a run ([log append](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/tools/registry/log-append.py:84), [required fields](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/tools/registry/log-append.py:99)).

Why it matters: The frozen instrument’s advertised raw output cannot be appended without an undocumented transformation that deletes and adds fields outside the pinned harness. The successful prereg dry-run validates the registry record, not this run-to-log integration.

### G1 still admits contradictory and non-integral count records

SEVERITY: freeze-blocking

Concrete mechanism: For control families, G1 checks only that covered buckets are zero and `ok <= n`; it never requires the sum of per-family `ok` values to equal run-level `n_control_refused_acceptable` ([l3](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/analysis/l3a_parse.py:325), [a5](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/analysis/a5_nl.py:309)). Setting every control-family `ok` to zero while leaving run-level acceptable refusal at 270/106 yielded `g1_counts=True`, `instrument_valid=True`, S1 PASS, and a reported acceptable-refusal rate of 1.0 in both scorers.

Additionally, `n_covered`, `n_control`, and `dev_n` are compared by numeric equality without `_count`, and `dev_parse_refused` is divided without any type or range check ([l3](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/analysis/l3a_parse.py:334), [a5](/home/ec2-user/css/kernel/kernel-of-truth/analysis/a5_nl.py:316)). Replacing each total with `600.0`/`855.0`, `270.0`/`106.0`, or `60.0` still produced a valid instrument; `dev_parse_refused=-1` and `0.5` also passed G4 and the whole instrument.

Why it matters: A dropped control-family classification or impossible/non-integral count can still support a nominal PASS. This contradicts the claimed fail-closed “full counts integrity” and leaves round-1 finding 6 materially open.
