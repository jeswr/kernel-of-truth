## 1. Key gaps (deduplicated, most-serious first)

### G1. The F2-line positive is licensed one notch too wide: "kernel-content-specific" is actually "correct-alignment-specific" (all four lenses; sharpest in A and C)

**GPT-5.6's point (lens A §"single weakest inferential link", lens B §"kernel-specific attribution", lens C K-NULL, lens D #1).** The shuffled-kernel derangement null destroys record↔item *identity*, not NSM *content*. Any aligned deterministic answer table — opaque IDs, a conventional key-value store, a plain typed schema — would produce the same lift and die under the same derangement. Lens A's constructive counterexample: a world where NSM explications add zero semantic value but every item has an authored canonical answer in a deterministic table passes every registered f2b control, including f2b-transfer. So the measured +0.1507 supports "an aligned deterministic acceptance rule plus retry beats these text/PRM arms on this authored task" — not "kernel semantics caused the lift". g1 (random-atom / Numberbatch nulls) does test content, but it rides f4's endpoint, not the f2b chain, and cannot rescue the f2b license.

**Affected:** f2b-replicate (verdict language), f2b-transfer (what a PASS licenses), f2 (secondaries), downstream a-h0/HE1 narration.

**Cross-reference:** the missing control is **NEW** — no backlog idea, no arm in any frozen or draft F-record, supplies a topology-and-cost-matched *aligned non-NSM store* null on the f2b chain. g1 is **ALREADY-PLANNED** (DRAFT, rides f4) but does not cover this. The claim-language half is a $0 correction, not an experiment.

**Designer take — adopt, with one sharpening GPT-5.6 missed.** The critique is correct and I endorse both halves: (a) relabel the f2b-replicate license programme-wide to "alignment-specific" now; (b) run the K-NULL ablation (lens C rank 1: canonical AST/record-ID verifier with vectors removed; normalized-text exact verifier; opaque-ID aligned table; shuffled; all at identical retry topology and FLOPs). The sharpening: on the F2 line the verifier's accept test is string-equality against canonical records — the *vectors* are plausibly not load-bearing in that seam at all, so parts of K-NULL may be near-no-ops mechanically. The decisive design step is therefore a **content-injection map**: enumerate exactly where kernel-derived bytes touch the model (option rendering; retry feedback, if any; nothing else?) before choosing K-NULL arms, or the ablation will "pass" trivially against arms that were never different. This folds naturally into lens C's ORACLE-FALL decomposition. One consolation the lenses under-state, worth keeping: even under the deflationary reading, the *efficiency* thesis survives in narrowed form — "a 135M model plus an authored deterministic answer key beats 1.7B-alone at lower FLOPs" is a real offload result whose open question becomes authoring cost (Law 1), not semantics. The NSM-content attribution matters for the correctness/semantics pitch, not for the offload economics.

- LOAD-BEARING: the f2b-replicate lift is real on fresh covered items in denominator-free form, 3 pinned seeds, R1 vs R3 [MEASURED: registry/verdicts/f2b-replicate + registry/assessments/f2b-replicate.json].

### G2. The circularity break in f2b-transfer is narrower than its headline: style/surface confounds survive blind adjudication, and the deranged-gloss probe only bounds gross mismatch (lenses A #1, D #3, C SURFACE-X)

**GPT-5.6's point.** Stage-1's endorsement statistic A can *discredit* transfer (low A) but a high A cannot distinguish "records are semantically right" from "item, options, and verifier share an authored surface cue that humans/LLMs also pick up". **Affected:** f2b-transfer (FROZEN, stage-1 in flight), f2b-transfer-llmproxy (FROZEN, running).

**Cross-reference:** **NEW.** Nothing in the backlog or the d-adj-t protocol crosses truth with style; provenance-blind rendering and the cannot-say escape do not bound this. **Designer take — adopt, timing-critical.** This is the best single catch in the review. It is CPU/adjudication-only, and it must be *frozen before f2b-transfer stage-2 unblinds* to retain confirmatory standing next to it. ### G3. Covered-slice conditioning is a *causal selection* problem (spectrum bias), not just a scope disclosure; and m0b is sense-blind (lenses A #2, B "representativeness", C COVERAGE-LOTTERY)

**GPT-5.6's point.** Coverage is not random: what is checkable plausibly correlates with canonical answerability, low ambiguity, easy distractors, and definitional shape — so conditional (covered-slice) accuracy can be inflated even when unconditional deployment value is ~0. Disclosure ("results bounded to the covered slice") does not fix a selection mechanism; only measurement does. m0b compounds it: token-mass lemma matching is sense-blind, so 0.3542 may overstate sense-correct expressibility. Proposed repairs: (i) a blind stratified representativeness audit (freeze external items and gold BEFORE mapping; compare covered vs uncovered strata on baseline difficulty, ambiguity, model-alone accuracy; report Δ_all = Pr(C)·Δ_C + Pr(¬C)·Δ_¬C); (ii) lens C's COVERAGE-LOTTERY — randomize *which items get a fixed-budget coverage-authoring attempt*, authors blind to baseline success, measure lift per author-hour by intent-to-treat.

**Affected:** m0b, every covered-slice verdict (f2b-*, e9-*), N1-LB/b-cov/LB-1.

**Cross-reference:** **ALREADY-PLANNED (partial).** The b-cov census is scoped and now-eligible (idea-leaderboard-benchmark-eval, status scoped, N1-LB in `docs/next/architecture-ladder.md` §10, bead hu10), and LB-1's design already mandates a selection-effect disclosure (model-alone accuracy checkable vs non-checkable) and the blended-identity Δ_B = κ_B·Δ_covered reporting. The *difficulty-stratified audit with pre-mapping freeze and the Δ_all decomposition* is a **NEW** extension of b-cov; COVERAGE-LOTTERY (randomized authoring ITT) is **NEW** and is the only proposal that makes *marginal coverage value* a measured causal quantity rather than an ingestion-plan assumption.

**Designer take — adopt the audit into b-cov's design now; hold the lottery.** The audit is cheap (CPU + bounded annotation) and converts the packet's own §7 worry into an instrument. The lottery is genuinely clever but its cost is dominated by author time, not the quoted $500–2k, and its lift-per-author-hour estimand needs a nontrivial per-item authoring success rate to power — sequence it after b-cov shows which benchmark has a workable yield. GPT-5.6's own honesty note is right: neither instrument licenses a coverage-growth slope. On m0b: the number was built and enveloped as a corpus-indexed *gate*, not a semantics claim — the record itself never claims sense-correctness — but a sense-audit sample inside the b-cov audit is a cheap upgrade and I endorse it.

## 2. Interesting areas to explore (reframings / higher-leverage directions)

1. **"Selective semantic compiler" framing (lens C).** Treat the kernel as converting *some* claims into versioned proof-carrying objects and refusing the rest; primary outcome = risk × coverage × cost including authoring. — **ALREADY-IN-BACKLOG (partial):** this is close to the linter's mode-S strict contract (idea-kernel-precision-linter, design node N-PL) plus first-class refusal (l3a). The *unifying value-frame* — one risk/coverage/cost ledger across verifier, engine, and linter — is **NEW** and I endorse it as prospectus language: it is more defensible than "better embeddings" and more honest than "hallucination-free" (a term the linter design already bans; **ALREADY-HANDLED**).

2. **Unbundle the object (lens C bet 1).** Independent hypotheses for primes / explications / vectors / hashes / axioms / refusal, so one component's pass cannot rhetorically spill onto the bundle. — **NEW as an artifact.** The seam taxonomy (A1–A6) partially does this structurally, but no document maps *which measured result exercises which component* (E5→vectors+adapter; f2b→records+alignment+retry; l3a/a5→engine+records; f1→pack format; nothing yet→primes-as-semantics). DECISION: build a one-page component-attribution matrix into the next prospectus revision; it makes G1's relabel systematic rather than ad hoc [STIPULATED: designer adoption of lens-C framing].

3. **Optimize marginal coverage value, not raw coverage (lens C bet 2).** Objective = expected corrected errors per author-hour, not lemma-touch or κ_B. — **ALREADY-IN-BACKLOG (partial):** idea-crosslingual-phrase-coverage already replaced raw frequency with an expected-token-savings objective on the efficiency side; the *correctness-side* analog is **NEW** and should reshape the coverage-growth-ingestion plan's Wave ordering once COVERAGE-LOTTERY-style data exists. Without measurement it stays a prioritization heuristic, not a claim.

4. **Versioned contradiction as the flagship correctness use case (lens C bet 3 + TIMEPACK).** Static world-knowledge QA fights ever-stronger RAG (Law 2 territory); versioned policies/APIs/world-packs are where deterministic refusal + content addressing are a *different capability*, not a better score. — **NEW.** Nothing in the backlog tests version pinning/rollback as an end-task capability; it also meets lens D's point that SERAC/IKE-class external-memory editors (unverified, model-supplied) are the true neighbours of f5/L2b, and the editing literature's evaluation dimensions (reliability, locality, sequential degradation, rollback) port directly. This is the most promising new *value surface* in the review; see §5 for sequencing.

5. **Prevention vs post-hoc detection (lens C POG).** Constrain generation to emit `{claim, record_hash, axiom_ids, query, status}` and render accepted claims deterministically, vs post-hoc linting. — **ALREADY-IN-BACKLOG (partial):** the components are idea-constrained-record-emission (grammar-constrained P10 emission, premise_eligible) + the N-PL renderer + L3c engine-in-decode; the *comparison* (prevention vs detection at matched compute) is a **NEW** and well-posed estimand.
