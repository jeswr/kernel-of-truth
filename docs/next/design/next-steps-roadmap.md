### Rank 1 — NLB-FW/1: natural-input reach on the family/world vertical

- **Question:** Can a frozen ≤360M constrained parser safely populate the rules/world interface from blind natural phrasings?
- **Why first:** it is the cheapest experiment that attacks the standing natural-input wall without LLM-proxy gold, and the family/world Tier-0 repair has no remaining a5-style structural ambiguity blocker.
- **Arms:** full two-tier parser plus contracts, ambiguity-set execution, and frozen abstention threshold as the sole primary arm; repaired Tier-0, confidence-only, and contract ablations as diagnostics; gold-parse ceiling.
- **Endpoints:** retention LB95 ≥0.90; unconditional answered-and-wrong UB95 <0.02; directional-stratum safety; fail-closed controls; parser bytes, latency, fall-through rate, and memory.
- **Cost:** first run NLB-0B, ≤5 GPU-hours and ≤$25; on GO, build the K=3 instrument for about $30–80 authoring and run the full gate within the existing ≤50 GPU-hour envelope. **[REGISTERED DESIGN]**
- **Ready to build:** NLB-0B on family/world is ready now. Full NLB-FW/1 freezes only after the pilot demonstrates ≥0.80 retention with ≤0.01 dangerous-wrong. **[READINESS: HIGH FOR PILOT]**
- **Stop rule:** pilot NO-GO returns to design; it does not consume a registered redesign cycle.

### Rank 2 — COV-XFER/1: coverage growth that earns an external-task result

- **Question:** Can coverage be expanded to at least two third-party benchmarks while retaining endorsed content, safe refusal, and measurable end-task lift?
- **Preparation:** add the genus-differentia `define` operator; run the $0 `b-cov` census; execute the cheapest signed ingestion wave; proceed to scoring only after ≥500 checkable items on each of two benchmarks and the registered κ thresholds are met.
- **Preferred targets:** definitional MMLU subjects plus the highest-yield second external benchmark selected mechanically by the census. OpenBookQA/ARC should not be forced into the first run unless the curated science world layer actually clears the gate.
- **Arms:** model alone; the surviving kernel mechanism; matched `knull`/plain authored store; generic retrieval/checker; shuffled content; gloss self-verify.
- **Primary endpoint:** paired external-gold accuracy lift on the highest-coverage benchmark. Co-primary licensing gates: coverage threshold on two benchmarks, runtime engagement matching the census, dangerous-wrong bound, and refusal correctness. Full-stream blended value is reported, never substituted for covered-slice accuracy.
- **Cost:** define-op and census approximately $0 CPU; first scored R1/R3 wave approximately $10–40 GPU, plus source/grammar engineering. **[DESIGN ESTIMATE]**
- **Ready to build:** define-op and census now; decisive scored run is blocked on measured yield and the exact front-end compatibility clearance. **[READINESS: PARTIAL]**
- **Stop rule:** if two benchmarks do not clear the coverage floor, report the measured trajectory and do not buy GPU evaluation.

### Rank 3 — SYS-XOVER/1: end-to-end quality–economics crossover

- **Question:** On task-originated natural inputs, does the best surviving small-host system reach the pure-neural frontier at lower fully charged lifecycle cost?
- **Arms:**
  - 135M model alone;
  - best matched pure-neural comparator from the frozen frontier, including 360M/1.7B as applicable;
  - small host plus matched generic retrieval/checking;
  - small host plus the winning rules/store/typing stack;
  - the same system with `knull` rules/content;
  - optional recovered-DDC arm only if DDC-R passes.
- **Quality co-primary:** full-stream accuracy non-inferior to the best pure-neural comparator at a frozen margin, with dangerous-wrong no worse. The denominator includes uncovered and abstained traffic.
- **Economic co-primary:** lower total cost at one or more predeclared workload horizons after charging parser/router bytes and latency, inference, retries, engine/store operations, training, mint/review/versioning, ontology alignment, and maintenance. Cost-per-correct and break-even query count are secondary diagnostics.
- **Attribution endpoint:** kernel-stack versus matched `knull` system at equal topology, budget, and coverage.
- **Cost:** approximately $20–60 for fresh latency/cost and any missing comparator cells if prior eligible outputs are reused; lifecycle measurement requires engineering time even where cash cost is low. **[DESIGN ESTIMATE]**
- **Ready to build:** ledger schema, frontier rules, and reuse manifest can be built now. The decisive run is blocked on a passed natural-input pin, coverage-cleared external tasks, final rules-source result, and any promoted human-valid typing shard. **[READINESS: DESIGN-NOW, RUN-LATER]**
- **Decision discipline:** neither a cheap engine nor a compressed model is an EFFICIENCY result unless this joint quality–cost gate clears.

## 5. Verdict-licensing map

A future **CORRECTNESS** verdict dossier would require, at minimum:

- valid rules-assisted host benefit or a clearly selected alternative mechanism;
- rules/content source attribution;
- human-valid typing if that layer remains in the system;
- a passed natural-input path with dangerous-error control;
- external-task coverage growth with endorsement and refusal discipline.

A future **EFFICIENCY** verdict dossier would additionally require:

- a full-stream accuracy comparison against the best pure-neural frontier;
- measured end-to-end latency, compute, bytes, retries, and routing;
- mint, review, training, versioning, ontology-alignment, and maintenance costs;
- coverage-adjusted value at predeclared workload horizons;
- kernel-versus-`knull` system attribution where a kernel-specific efficiency claim is sought.

DDC-R or RULES-2 may supply stronger efficient-system arms, but neither substitutes for SYS-XOVER’s end-to-end economic gate. **[DESIGN CONSTRAINT]**
