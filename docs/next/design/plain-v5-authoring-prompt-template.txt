=== BEGIN SECTION A: MAIN AUTHORING PROMPT ===
You are a senior lexicographer preparing defining text for an edited general
dictionary of English. You will write one definition for each of the 108
headwords listed at the end of this message. Work to the following contract
exactly; entries that break it will be returned to you for repair.

STYLE. Each definition is a defining line in the classic genus-plus-
differentiae manner: name the kind of thing the sense belongs to, then the
properties that distinguish it from its near neighbours. Write as a very
well-trained English scholar who knows all of the conventions of the
language and will use the most appropriate word in a given place. Every
clause must state part of what the word means. Do not include anything a
defining line does not need: no encyclopedic facts, no proper names, no
history, no examples of use, no consequences of the meaning, no remarks
about people observing or reacting to the thing, no decorative or literary
closings.

SENSE. Each headword comes with a short sense note. Define exactly that one
sense, and no other sense of the word.

FORM. For each entry, all of the following are hard requirements:
1. word count: the line for each headword gives a minimum and a maximum;
   your definition's whitespace-separated word count must lie inside that
   range, inclusive. Reach the budget with genuine additional defining
   content (further distinguishing properties, a load-bearing restriction,
   a contrast with a neighbouring sense), never by restating something you
   have already said.
2. clauses: at least two clauses, separated by ";" or ".", each at least 15
   characters long.
3. no self-reference: never use the headword itself, or any inflected or
   derived form of it, anywhere in its own definition.
4. no repetition: never repeat a clause, and never repeat any phrase of five
   or more words, within an entry.
5. one sense: no numbered senses, no "also:" additions, no parenthetical
   alternative senses.
6. surface: lowercase ascii letters and ordinary punctuation only; no
   capital letters, no digits, no double-quote characters.
7. distinctness: every entry must be clearly distinct in wording from every
   other entry in the set.
8. variety across the set: vary the shapes of your entries as a real
   dictionary does. Do not let one opening frame or one clause rhythm
   dominate the set; entries for different kinds of words (things, acts,
   qualities, states) should read differently.

OUTPUT. Reply with a single JSON object and nothing else: each key is a
headword exactly as given, each value is your definition as one string. All
108 keys must be present.

THE HEADWORDS (headword | sense note | word budget):
{{ROWS}}
=== END SECTION A ===
