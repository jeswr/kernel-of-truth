## 0. Verdict in three lines

1. **Continuation sets: the engine does not emit them — measured no as an
   interface; measured yes as a substrate.** No continuation, enumeration, or
   trace API was found by a name-pattern search over `Engine` attributes and
   function defs in `kot_axiom.py` (§2.3 states exactly what that search can
   and cannot establish), and a 90-line external adapter over six public
   engine attributes derives EXACT admissible-continuation sets at
   28 µs–368 ms per partial query on the pinned stores (the 368 ms extreme is
   the 72,839-candidate define enumeration) [MEASURED: probe legs
   0+3, sha256 6990c047baa3845d].
2. **Derivation traces: partially emitted, and the closure measurement is
   FILE-granular.** Every accept carries a two-field certificate (provenance
   world-record ids + license axiom refs); replaying it on a store restricted
   to the cited world records plus the ENTIRE cited axiom FILES (the probe
   strips the `#constraint` suffix — §2.2) reproduces the answer for
   **464/600** covered l3a answers and **438/855** covered a5 answers, and
   systematically fails for the rest (the certificate omits the
   licensing-support class assertions and the class-licensing axioms it
   consumed). Constraint-granular closure is TBD until B1 (§5). Refusals emit
   **no** certificate at all (`{status, code, reason}` prose only)
   [MEASURED (file-granular): probe leg 2, sha256 6990c047baa3845d].
3. **Consequence: the NTP §8 gate is NOT cleared today; for the consumers it
   CAN serve (lookup-derivation traces, structured diagnostics, formal-field
   continuations — NOT proof states or search transitions, §3), clearing it
   is a bounded, CPU-only build** (§5) with a mechanical, now-exhaustive
   acceptance contract. The GPT-5.6 review authorizes the B1+B3
   implementation spike after this revision; no trace-, distillation-, or
   mask-consuming model experiment is budgeted before P3-E-NTP-GATE-0 passes
   (ASM-0981, ASM-0982 as amended by ASM-1024).

## 1. What was probed, and why this operationalisation

- PREMISE: the engine under test is the unmodified production engine
  `tools/axiom/kot_axiom.py` (five ops: unique/lookup/count/instance/define;
  fail-closed ERR_* refusals; CWA store validation) plus the code-vertical
  oracle `tools/axiom/kot_code.py`, built exactly as the frozen l3a/a5
  instruments build them, over the pinned corpora `data/axioms-v0` +
  `data/world-v0`, `data/code-axioms-v0` + `data/code-world-v0`, and the
  definitional substrate (`data/axioms-definitional-v0` + onto-obo shards +
  mint bridge) [MEASURED: poc/ntp-gate/probe.py build calls; output sha256
  6990c047baa3845d].
- PREMISE: "continuation set" and "replayable derivation trace" are
  operationalised at the consuming-arm interface — G1/G2 per-field
  admissible-value sets for partial queries, and replay-closure of the
  emitted certificate, respectively [STIPULATED: ASM-0980; clause 2's replay
  implementation is amended to constraint granularity by ASM-1020, §2.2].
- The probe (`poc/ntp-gate/probe.py`, stdlib-only, RNG-free, doubled-pass
  byte-identical) has four legs: (0) a name-pattern API-absence search (§2.3
  for its limits), (1) emission inventory over the full pinned eval sets
  (900 l3a + 977 a5 queries), (2) certificate replay for every covered
  answer — file-granular, see §2.2, (3) worked continuation partials on both
  verticals (seven partials; NOT a coverage contract — §5.4 adds the
  exhaustive one), (4) trace-gap demonstrations. All outputs land in
  `poc/ntp-gate/out/`.
- What replay tests, honestly stated: replay re-runs the SAME engine
  implementation on a restricted store, so it measures internal
  self-consistency of the emission — sufficiency of the cited subset — not an
  independently checkable proof object, and not minimality (a certificate
  citing the whole store would trivially pass). That is the right bar for an
  API/interface gate; it is NOT audit-grade proof language, and this revision
  strikes such language throughout [STIPULATED: ASM-1028].

- The define-op emits the same certificate shape (provenance = onto-obo

- Raw latency context, this box (2 shared cores, single-thread CPython, per

### 2.2 Certificate replay — is the emission a closed derivation trace? [MEASURED — FILE-granular, sha256 6990c047baa3845d, leg 2]

Replay = rebuild a store from ONLY the records the answer names (provenance
world ids + license axiom refs), re-run the same query, require the same
value (ASM-0980 clause 2).

**Granularity correction (review technical error #1, accepted).** License
refs have the form `<corpus>/<file>.json#<constraint-index>`; the probe's
`replay_certificate` (probe.py ~line 118) strips the `#constraint` suffix and
reloads the ENTIRE axiom file. Several axiom files hold multiple constraints,
so a replay can succeed by consuming an uncited sibling constraint from the
same file. The implemented replay is therefore **file-granular, not
record/constraint-granular**, and "exactly the records it names" (this note's
rev-1 wording) was false as a description of the implementation. Consequences,
relabelled:

- **File-granular closure = 464/600 (77.3%) l3a, 438/855 (51.2%) a5**
  [MEASURED: sha256 6990c047baa3845d, leg 2].
- **Constraint-granular closure = TBD after B1** — the file-granular numbers
  are UPPER BOUNDS on it (a constraint-granular replay can only remove
  successes, never add them), and may contain false-positive replays
  [STIPULATED: ASM-1020].
- The FAILURE analysis below is unaffected: a replay that fails file-granular
  fails a fortiori constraint-granular, so the 136 + 417 systematic failures
  and their family attribution stand as measured.

| vertical | covered answers | replay exact (FILE-granular) | replay fails | failing families (all of the family, refusal observed) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| l3a family/world | 600 | **464 (77.3%)** | 136 | count-maker 43/43 `ERR_UNLICENSED_COUNT`; unique-maker 43/43 `ERR_UNLICENSED_UNIQUE`; instance-true 50/50 `ERR_TERM_UNLICENSED` |
| a5 code | 855 | **438 (51.2%)** | 417 | where-defined 201/201 `ERR_UNLICENSED_UNIQUE`; instance-true 216/216 `ERR_TERM_UNLICENSED` |
| define (spot, 3 subjects) | 3 | 3 | 0 | — (but see bridge caveat below) |

The failures are systematic, not noisy, and each names a missing record
class the derivation consumed but the emission does not cite:

1. **Licensing-support class assertions.** Cardinality-licensed unique/count
   answers require `subject ∈ classes[subj_class]`; that class-assertion
   world record is consulted but never enters `provenance` (families
   count-maker, unique-maker, where-defined — 287 answers).
2. **Class-licensing axioms.** `instance` TRUE answers emit
   `license: ["asserted"]`, citing no axiom file at all, although the
   concept's admission to `licensed_classes` (via some disjoint/range/domain/
   cardinality axiom) was a precondition — replay refuses
   `ERR_TERM_UNLICENSED` (266 answers).
3. **Refusals carry nothing.** A refusal names a code and prose; it cites no
   record, no violation object, no terminating step — there is no reject
   trace to replay, and (review technical error #2, accepted) no reject
   REPLAY SEMANTICS existed in this design either: probe `replay_leg`
   (~line 136) skips refusals and non-covered records entirely. §5.3 now pins
   the reject contracts.
4. **Define caveat:** the 3/3 define replays consumed the pinned mint bridge
   from the corpus, which the certificate does not name — the definitional
   certificate is closed only modulo that pinned artifact.

### 2.3 Continuation sets — no API found by name-pattern search; exact sets derivable [MEASURED: sha256 6990c047baa3845d, legs 0+3]

- **What leg 0 actually is (review technical error #6, accepted):** a
  name-pattern search — regex
  `continu|legal|admiss|enum|next_|trace|proof|deriv|expla` over `Engine`
  attribute names and `def` names in `kot_axiom.py` — returning zero hits.
  This establishes that no API under any CONVENTIONAL name exists on the
  axiom engine; it does not comprehensively inspect the code-oracle surface
  (`kot_code.py` was not swept), and it cannot prove absence under arbitrary
  naming. Rev-1's phrase "mechanised inspection found zero matching
  attributes or defs — the engine has no continuation/trace surface of any
  kind" overclaimed; the operative, honest statement is: no such surface is
  documented, none was found by pattern, and `query()` is all-or-nothing on
  every observed call [MEASURED: leg 0 + leg 1 shapes, sha256
  6990c047baa3845d].
- The probe's `ContinuationAdapter` (~90 lines, consuming only the public
  attributes `licensed_rels`, `licensed_classes`, `entities`,
  `defn_licensed`, `defn`, `query`) derives exact G1 (licensed-vocabulary)
  and G2 (answer-admissible) sets. Note: "public attributes" here means
  mutable CPython fields, not a versioned interface contract — B2 replaces
  this substrate access with a versioned surface (§5.4). Worked partials:

| partial | next field | G1 | G2 | engine calls | wall |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `{}` (which ops answerable) | op | 5 | 5 | 9,229 | 102 ms |

| `{op:unique, rel:mother, dir:fwd}` | subject | 324 | 122 | 324 | 2.0 ms |

- These seven partials are DEMONSTRATIONS, not a continuation contract: only

- **Field-level sets are NOT token-level masks.** The G1→G2 gap (324→122;

### 2.4 Trace gaps — computed internally, never emitted [MEASURED: sha256 6990c047baa3845d, leg 4]

- The CWA pass builds structured violation objects
  `{code, detail, implicated}` (6 in the l3a store, 3 planted in a5), but an
  `ERR_CONFLICT` refusal names none of them: the probe shows the refusal for
  query q0831 side-by-side with the exactly-matching in-memory
  `VIOLATION_FUNCTIONAL` object it will not cite.
- inverseOf canonicalisation flips are invisible: q0461 answers a `lookup`
  through the canonical-partner flip and nothing in the emission records
  that the store direction consulted was the reverse of the queried one.
- Refusals do not name the terminating step of the pre-registered validation
  order (shape → term licensing → entity → conflict → op license → records),
  although the code path is deterministic — the code-to-step mapping exists
  in source only.

## 4. Why this is the right bar (and not a stricter one)

- PREMISE: the dependent arms consume, at minimum: admissible-value sets at
  the formal-query interface (P3 mask substrate), self-contained derivations
  for curricula and accept-audit (P6, H-VL), and machine-readable failure
  operands for retry training (H-VL, RLEF-shape feedback) [STIPULATED:
  ASM-0980; the arm-side requirements restate round1-critique-synthesis
  A.8/A.9 and NTP.md §5–6].
- A stricter bar (theorem-prover-grade proof objects, search-tree
  transitions) tests capability no v0-registered arm consumes and the v0
  engine was never specified to have — and per §3 / ASM-1023 the arms that
  WOULD need it are simply not cleared by this gate. A weaker bar ("the
  engine answers and refuses deterministically") is what l3a/a5 already
  measured and is exactly the assumption NTP.md flagged as insufficient.
  Replay-closure is the weakest property that makes a trace *checkable by
  reconstruction* — with the explicit limitation (ASM-1028) that
  same-implementation replay establishes self-consistency, not an
  independent proof object; independent checking would need a second
  implementation or external verifier and is NOT claimed or required here.

## 5. The build: what closes the gate, priced [STIPULATED: ASM-0982, amended by ASM-1020–1024, ASM-1027]

Three additive, opt-in, CPU-only engine extensions; the default `query()`
emission stays byte-identical AND resource-equivalent (§5.6) — the frozen
l3a/a5 instruments consume it.

### 5.1 Dependency taxonomy — what "every consulted record" means [STIPULATED: ASM-1022]

Review technical error #3, accepted: engine init scans the full corpora,
builds indexes (`rel_fwd`, `classes`, licensing sets, `defn`), and computes
global CWA violations, so "every consulted record" is undefined without a
boundary — a literal complete trace could be store-sized. B1 traces
therefore distinguish, per step:

- **Query-local witness records** — records whose CONTENT this query's
  evaluation consulted: the matched world record(s); the licensing-support
  class-assertion record(s) (§2.2 item 1); the specific axiom CONSTRAINT(s)
  whose license the answer exercises, incl. the class-admitting constraint
  for `instance` (§2.2 item 2); consulted mint-bridge entries (§2.2 item 4);
  for positive-witness refusals, the implicated violation records (§5.3).
  These are ENUMERATED in the trace by stable id.
- **Build-time/global dependencies** — the index structures, licensing sets,
  and the global CWA scan. These are NOT enumerated per-trace; they are
  referenced once by the trace's **store snapshot digest** (sha256 over the
  canonicalised store inputs: sorted axiom corpus files + world records +
  bridge) plus the engine version. A trace's absence-type claims are relative
  to that digest (§5.3).

### 5.2 The trace schema `kot-trace/1` [STIPULATED: ASM-1024; answers review technical error #7]

Every `query(q, trace=True)` emission carries:

| field | content |
|---|---|
| `schema` | literal `"kot-trace/1"` + integer minor version; consumers MUST fail closed on unknown major version; within a major version changes are additive-only |
| `engine` | engine source id + ALGORITHM-version pin |
| `store_snapshot` | sha256 digest per §5.1 — the anchor for all build-time/global deps and absence claims |
| `query` | canonicalised query echo (post-desugaring for the code vertical, with the pre-desugar form retained) |
| `steps` | ordered list over the CLOSED step enum `{shape, term-licensing, entity, conflict, op-license, records}` — the pre-registered validation order (§2.4); each entry `{step, outcome, consulted:[...]}` |
| `consulted` refs | stable identifiers only: world-record id; axiom constraint ref `<corpus>/<file>.json#<constraint-index>` (the `#` suffix is LOAD-BEARING — constraint-granular, §5.5a); mint-bridge entry id |
| `outcome` vocabulary | closed enum: per-step `pass` / `refuse:<ERR_*>`; terminal `answer` / `refuse:<ERR_*>` — no free text in machine fields (prose `reason` retained separately for humans) |
| `canonicalisation` | inverseOf/direction flips actually taken (§2.4), emitted whenever consulted direction ≠ queried direction |
| `rejection_contract` | for refusals: `positive-witness` or `snapshot-relative` per the §5.3 assignment table |

### 5.3 B1 — trace emission, accepts AND rejects [STIPULATED: ASM-1021 (reject contracts), ASM-1022, ASM-1024]

`query(q, trace=True)`: the §5.2 object, for every accept and every refusal.
~150–300 lines + tests (repriced, §5.7).

**Accept replay semantics (constraint-granular — ASM-1020):** rebuild a
store from EXACTLY the trace-named query-local witnesses — the cited world
records, the cited constraint(s) ONLY (an axiom file is sliced to its cited
constraint indexes; uncited sibling constraints in the same file are
EXCLUDED), and the cited bridge entries — re-run the query, require the same
value.

**Reject replay semantics — two contracts, pinned (review technical error
#2):** a restricted-record replay cannot prove absence, so every ERR_* code
is assigned by B1 to exactly one contract class; the assignment table is part
of the `kot-trace/1` spec and P3-E-NTP-GATE-0 verifies every observed refusal
under its assigned contract [STIPULATED: ASM-1021]:

- **Positive-witness rejections** (violation-backed refusals, e.g.
  `ERR_CONFLICT`): the trace cites the implicated store-violation record ids
  (§2.4's `{code, detail, implicated}` objects). Replay = rebuild a store
  restricted to the cited witness records (+ their licensing constraints),
  require the SAME refusal code at the SAME terminating step. The witness is
  self-contained: it replays without the full store.
- **Snapshot-relative rejections** (absence-type refusals, e.g.
  `ERR_NO_RECORD`, unknown-entity, `ERR_TERM_UNLICENSED`,
  `ERR_UNLICENSED_*` — conclusions that depend on a complete store/index
  boundary): the trace pins the `store_snapshot` digest + the terminating
  step + the consulted index family. Replay = re-run the query against the
  digest-verified FULL snapshot, require the identical code and terminating
  step. The claim licensed is "absent FROM THIS SNAPSHOT", never absolute
  absence; no restricted-store absence proof is attempted or implied.

### 5.4 B2 — continuation API [STIPULATED: ASM-1024]

`continuations(partial)`: the ASM-0980 G1/G2 sets, derived analytically from
the engine's existing indexes rather than by query loops; the probe's
brute-force adapter is the frozen reference semantics, equivalence-tested.
B2 must specify semantics for EVERY op, every legal partial, every refusal
interaction, the code-oracle desugaring, and — new in this revision — an
**invalid-prefix contract**: a partial that is out-of-grammar (unknown op,
unknown/out-of-order field, value outside the preceding field's G1
vocabulary) draws a fail-closed, deterministic `ERR_CONT_*` refusal from a
closed code set — NEVER a silent empty set, so "no admissible continuation"
(legal prefix, empty G2) and "illegal prefix" are machine-distinguishable.
The versioned surface is `kot-cont/1` (same schema/version/compatibility
discipline as §5.2); the adapter's raw-attribute access pattern is a probe
artefact, not the contract. ~150–300 lines + tests (repriced, §5.7). The
72,839-candidate define continuation return gets a pinned serialization
budget: bytes and wall measured and reported under acceptance, feeding the
§6.2 cost hooks.

### 5.5 B3 — structured refusal diagnostics

Refusals gain machine operands — terminating step, contract class, and for
positive-witness refusals stable references into the store-violation objects
(§2.4). ~60–120 lines.

### 5.6 Acceptance — all mechanical, all required [STIPULATED: ASM-0982 as amended by ASM-1020, ASM-1021, ASM-1024, ASM-1027]

(a) **Constraint-granular accept replay closure 100%** on both pinned eval
    sets, consuming trace-named records under the §5.3 accept semantics
    (file-sliced to cited constraint indexes). Baseline for comparison:
    464/600, 438/855 — FILE-granular, hence an upper-bound baseline; the
    constraint-granular baseline is additionally reported by the re-pointed
    probe before the build lands, so the gate readout states both
    granularities [STIPULATED: ASM-1020].
(b) **Reject replay 100%** on all observed refusals (422/422 across both
    eval sets), each under its assigned contract (§5.3): positive-witness
    refusals replay restricted; snapshot-relative refusals replay
    digest-verified full-snapshot with identical code + terminating step
    [STIPULATED: ASM-1021].
(c) **B2 ≡ reference adapter EXHAUSTIVELY over the closed grammar**, not on
    seven partials: for every op, every legal prefix depth, and every legal
    value assignment of the prefix fields (values drawn from the G1

    no silent caps). MUST include: the `define` candidate-continuation case,

    `callers-of`), and the invalid-prefix contract (every `ERR_CONT_*` mode

(d) **Byte-identical default emissions** pre/post on both frozen
