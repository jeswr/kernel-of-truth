## 1. Question and position

RULES-1 tests the engine at the **memory slot, inference time**: a deterministic,
proof-carrying reasoner derives entailed-but-never-stated facts per prompt
(temporary world) and couples to the frozen host via verify-retry (A3). RULES-2
tests the **train-time slot**: materialise the DEFAULT world's closure — the
persistent world whose entailments may, per the maintainer's own split, be
computed once at build/train time [STIPULATED: ASM-1139] — as **training data**,
fine-tune a small host on it, and ask whether the model **internalises the
inference**, i.e. answers entailed queries **with no engine at inference time**.

This is the EFFICIENCY angle of the programme: RULES-1's A4 arm prices the
default world as a *lookup* ledger; RULES-2 asks whether the same materialised
closure, pushed through fine-tuning, becomes a *rule in the weights* — and at
what training + inference cost relative to (a) the plain model and (b) RULES-1's
engine-at-inference.

**Grounding in the two measured anchors.**

- **DECONF-A1 (runtime inert):** C_dec = 1.0 — every decision of the old
  structured engine was reproducible from a flat projection of the store's
  STATED bytes [MEASURED: `poc/deconf-a1/audit_a1.py` certificate].
- **RULES-1 certificate (engine non-inert):** the RULES-1 engine's decisions on
  ENTAILED cells are reproducible from **no** projection of the stated bytes —
  C_dec(stated) = 1.0 (1,716/1,716), C_dec(entailed) = 0.0 (0/3,680), engine
  858/858 vs third-party CLUTRR gold, Wilson-LB95 0.9955 ≥ 0.98
  [MEASURED: `poc/rules-1/results/certificate-result.json`, sha `e0071e9e…`,
  registered $0 CPU re-run 2026-07-11; claim capped at *machinery non-inert*
  pending the phase-2 knull ablation, per ASM-1138].

RULES-2 runs the same audit logic **one level up**: the training corpus now
CONTAINS the entailments (materialised by the non-inert engine), so the honest
question is no longer "can the bytes produce the answer" — they can, trivially —
but "did the model learn the **rule** or the **lookup table**?" The instrument
for that question is the **train-bytes projection** (§4, control c8): the
DECONF-A1 projection family applied to the pinned TRAINING corpus. A fine-tuned
model whose out-of-world answers are reproducible by flat lookup over its own
training bytes has internalised nothing; the claim attaches only where lookup
fails and the model still answers correctly.

**The known hazard, named up front.** The train slot was deferred from RULES-1
precisely because it carries a memorisation-vs-rule confound needing its own
controls, and because materialised entailments in training data can *degrade*
downstream performance [LIT-BACKED: Herron et al. 2025, cited in WMRE-1 §2.2;
llm-integration survey d-train assessment]. Both hazards get named controls
below (c7 split, c8 projection, the degradation guard); neither is assumed away.

## 2. Design

### 2.1 Worlds, license discipline, and the memorisation split

The train/eval split is built so that **string-level memorisation cannot produce
the primary endpoint**, and so that no CC-BY-NC bytes enter training.

| Role | World | Regime | License |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Training default world** | `data/world-v0/` (598 records / 324 entities, Presley anchor) + a seeded synthetic expansion generated by the `nsk1-eval` family generator with FRESH seeds and families disjoint from the eval twin | persistent DEFAULT world; closure materialised once at build time by the pinned RULES-1 twin engine | kernel-native / redistributable |
| **In-world held-out cells** | a fixed 10% of the training worlds' entailed cells, excluded from the corpus by pinned split | default world, held out | same |
| **Out-of-world eval (PRIMARY)** | `data/nsk1-clutrr/` (958 items, per-item fresh entity names, third-party gold predating the kernel) | TEMPORARY worlds: facts supplied in-prompt at eval, **no engine anywhere** | CC-BY-NC-4.0, **eval-side only** [STIPULATED: ASM-1125; PROPOSED-ASM-1421] |
| Redistributable eval twin | `data/nsk1-eval/` (1,000 + 100 control) | anything redistributed reports here | redistributable |

Three eval strata, reported separately and never pooled
[STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1436]:

- **S-mem** (in-world, trained facts): sanity anchor; success here is lookup,
  claims nothing.
- **S-held** (in-world, held-out entailed cells): partial-generalisation
  diagnostic; correlated entities remain, so it is descriptive only.
- **S-out** (out-of-world, fresh-name CLUTRR items presented as prompt-supplied
  facts): **the only stratum that carries the internalisation claim.** Fresh
  per-item entity names (native to nsk1-clutrr) guarantee zero string overlap
  with training; the model must APPLY the rule to novel facts in a temporary
  world it has never seen — the exact regime RULES-1's engine serves at
  inference, now served (or not) by the weights.

### 2.2 Training corpus

Generated CPU-only, deterministic, ~$0, by a materialisation pass over the
training default world using the **pinned RULES-1 twin engine**
(`twin_engine.py` sha `399fcd8d…`, rules.n3 sha `9857da89…`, corpus digests
verbatim from the frozen rules-1 record — fail-closed pin verification, no
re-derivation) [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1437]. Per example: stated facts
rendered in the RULES-1 canonical verbalisation, a query, and a target. Four
example families [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1422]:

1. **Stated** (S): query answered by a stated fact — format exposure.
2. **Entailed** (Cl(S)\S): the treatment content — E1 cover-elimination, E2
   domain/range typing, E3 property-chain grandparent targets, each carrying
   its `why()` provenance in a sidecar (never in the training text for B2;
   see B3).
3. **Refusal** (E5): insufficient-premise queries with the named-refusal target
   ("cannot be determined from these facts") — fail-closed behaviour is
   TRAINED and SCORED, not assumed [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1431].
4. **Proof-augmented** variant (B3 corpus only): family 2 with the one-line
   proof chain rendered before the answer (rationale distillation).

Corpus sizes ~20k–40k examples; every corpus pinned by sha; the generator takes
an explicit seed (estate convention). Provenance sidecar retains regime tags
{owl-rl, horn-def, policy} per ASM-1162 so the readout can stratify
internalisation by regime (does the policy-regime R-COVER-ELIM internalise as
readily as owl-rl chains?).

### 2.3 Arms

Hosts: **R1 = SmolLM2-135M-Instruct, R2 = SmolLM2-360M-Instruct** (revisions
pinned at freeze from the estate's pinned records); two rungs, SIGN-ONLY
language [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1433]. Fine-tuning: LoRA, pinned
hyperparameters (rank/lr/epochs frozen at prereg; any HP search confined to a
synthetic dev split, disclosed, never touching eval) [STIPULATED:
PROPOSED-ASM-1432]. Eval decoding: greedy, fp32, seeds {0,1,2}; fine-tune seeds
{0,1,2} crossed with eval where affordable, else per-arm 3 FT seeds × 1 eval
pass with byte-identical repeat [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1439].

| Arm | Contents | Claim it carries |
|---|---|---|
| **B0** | plain model, no fine-tune, no engine (RULES-1 A1 prompt format, token-matched) | floor |
| **B1** | fine-tuned on families 1+3 only (stated + refusal; NO entailments) | training-exposure control — separates "saw the format/domain" from "saw the inference" |
| **B2** | fine-tuned on families 1+2+3 (stated + **materialised closure** + refusal) | **the treatment: internalisation claim, S-out only** |
| B3 (optional) | as B2 but family-2 examples carry one-line proofs | rationale-distillation variant, descriptive |
| **B4** | RULES-1 A3 verbatim (frozen host + engine verify-retry k=4 at inference) on the identical S-out slice | engine-at-inference comparator — the gap to close |
| B5 | R3 = SmolLM2-1.7B alone (no fine-tune), reused/re-run on the S-out slice | big-model efficiency comparator |
| c1′ | fine-tuned on a **Sattolo-deranged closure**: entailment targets shuffled across items at identical corpus size/token count | content control — lift must collapse [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1426] |
| c7 | the three-strata split itself (S-mem / S-held / S-out reported separately) | memorisation-vs-rule diagnostic [PROPOSED-ASM-1436] |
| **c8** | **train-bytes projection**: mechanical flat lookup over the pinned training corpus (DECONF-A1's GS projection family applied to training bytes), answering every eval query by exact/aligned retrieval | the internalisation instrument gate — S-out must NOT be lookup-recoverable [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1427] |
| c4 | abstain-all / answer-all trivial floors | L3a discipline |

**A6 (decode-time logit mask) stays deferred** even though DV-5 parked it "to
RULES-2": RULES-2's answer surface is the same closed 23-word kinship
vocabulary, so the tautology (\|L\| = 1 ⇒ the mask IS the engine's answer in
model skin) is unchanged; the \|L\| ledger remains mandatory if it ever runs on
an open surface [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1435, amends ASM-1137 notes].

**Claim assignment** [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1423]: B2's accuracy on S-mem is
lookup-in-weights — the weights analog of A4's runtime-inert lookup (ASM-1139)
— and is reported as a memorisation descriptive, never an inference claim. Only
S-out, with c8 failing to reproduce it and c1′ collapsing, supports
"internalised the inference".

### 2.4 Endpoints and decision rules

**Instrument gates (all precede any interpretation):**

- **G1 pin gate:** every RULES-1 pin verified byte-exact (twin, rules.n3,
  corpus digests, certificate sha); fail closed [PROPOSED-ASM-1437].
- **G2 c8 lookup gate:** the train-bytes projection must score ≈ chance on
  S-out (fresh names ⇒ retrieval finds nothing); if c8 recovers S-out above a
  pre-registered ceiling, the split is broken ⇒ **INSTRUMENT-INVALID, stop
  before interpreting any arm** [PROPOSED-ASM-1427].
- **G3 headroom gate:** acc(B0, S-out) ≤ 0.85 (saturated floor cannot measure
  a lift).
- **G4 repeat gate:** byte-identical eval repeat per arm; FT-seed variance
  disclosed.

**Primary (one endpoint):** **B2 − B0 on S-out entailed cells vs the pinned
third-party CLUTRR gold**; paired item BCa bootstrap B = 10⁴, one-sided 95%
LB > 0 [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1424].
**KILL-d: LB ≤ 0** — fine-tuning on the materialised closure does not
internalise the inference; the closure remains a data product; the engine
remains required at inference; route back to the RULES-1 memory-slot
architecture as the standing position. No model claim.

**Holm-corrected secondaries:**

- **s1′ content:** c1′ shuffled-closure recovery UB95 < 0.30 — the lift must be
  destroyed by content destruction at identical bulk [PROPOSED-ASM-1426].
- **s2′ exposure:** B2 − B1 LB > 0 on S-out — the lift must exceed what
  format/domain exposure alone buys [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1425].
- **s3′ gap closure:** noninferiority of B2 (no engine, k = 1) vs B4
  (engine-at-inference, k = 4) on S-out, absolute margin δ = 0.05, sign-only
  language; **conditional** on RULES-1's A3 lift having been positive (else
  unevaluable: null, never a fail). Descriptive companion: gap fraction
  (B2−B0)/(B4−B0) [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1428].
- **s4′ degradation guard (Herron):** acc(B2) on stated cells and E5 refusal
  correctness each within 0.05 of B0/B1; breach flags SIDE-EFFECT and caps any
  deployment-shaped language, without touching the primary [STIPULATED:
  PROPOSED-ASM-1430].

**Descriptives:** S-mem / S-held / S-out profile per arm (the c7 curve); regime
stratification (owl-rl vs policy internalisation); proof-depth stratification
(≤ 4, per ASM-1160); B3 − B2 (does proof text help?); the full efficiency
vector below.

**Verdict rules (sketch for the kot-reg/2 draft):** INSTRUMENT-INVALID on
¬(G1∧G2∧G3∧G4); FAIL on KILL-d; PASS on primary ∧ s1′ ∧ s2′; else INCONCLUSIVE.

### 2.5 The efficiency trade — measured, not asserted

RULES-2 fills the `training_compute` slot RULES-1 left at 0. The pre-registered
ledger [STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1429]:

- **Training side:** measured LoRA GPU-h, $ and a FLOPs formula per arm/rung;
  one-off closure-materialisation CPU cost (RULES-1 datum: full pass < 2 s on
  2 shared cores [MEASURED: `poc/rules-1/RESULT.md`]).
- **Inference side:** B2 = one forward pass, k = 1, zero engine dependency;
  B4 = forward passes up to k = 4 + engine CPU (L3a class 5.3 µs/query
  [MEASURED: `registry/verdicts/l3a.json`]) + the deployment dependency itself.
- **Break-even:** N\* = training cost ÷ per-query saving, reported with the
  honest pre-registered caveat that the engine's marginal compute is
  microseconds on CPU, so the arithmetic may well show the engine cheaper at
  any realistic N — in which case the ledger says so and the efficiency case
  for the train slot rests only on the k = 4 → k = 1 resample reduction and on
  removing the engine as a deployment dependency. **This document does not
  presume which way the ledger falls.**
- **V-vector:** accuracy / params / memory / inference_compute /
  training_compute per arm, same field layout as the rules-1 record.

## 3. Sequencing gate

Per ASM-1136 as proposed, RULES-2 is **gated on RULES-1's GPU readout**
[STIPULATED: PROPOSED-ASM-1420]. rules-1 is FROZEN (sha `0ef03ee1…`) with the
certificate precondition PASSED, but the GPU campaign has **no verdict yet**
(`registry/verdicts/` has no rules-1 entry at time of writing). Concretely:

- **RULES-1 PASS** (A3 lift LB > 0, s1 collapse): RULES-2 proceeds as designed;
  s3′ (gap closure) is live.
- **RULES-1 KILL-b** (LB ≤ 0): the "close the gap" framing is void; RULES-2 as
  written does NOT auto-run — it returns to the maintainer for explicit
  re-authorization under a changed rationale ("can train time succeed where the
  inference-time coupling failed?"), with s3′ struck and the primary retained.
- **RULES-1 INSTRUMENT-INVALID / INCONCLUSIVE:** RULES-2 blocked pending the
  instrument fix; no spend.

This gate is itself a maintainer decision point (MD-R2-1), since the maintainer
may reasonably want the train-slot answer regardless of the memory-slot result.

## 4. Maintainer decision points

Options listed; recommendations only; **nothing is decided here**.

**MD-R2-1 — Sequencing.** (a) Gate RULES-2 strictly on RULES-1 PASS
(ASM-1136 verbatim; KILL-b ⇒ explicit re-authorization required). (b) Run
RULES-2 regardless of RULES-1's readout (the train slot answers an independent
question). **Recommendation: (a)** — it is the registered sequencing logic and
avoids paying for the follow-on before the engine's derivations are shown to
matter anywhere. [PROPOSED-ASM-1420]

**MD-R2-2 — Training-world composition.** (a) world-v0 + fresh-seed synthetic
expansion (disjoint from nsk1-eval), nsk1-clutrr strictly eval-side. (b) Add
nsk1-eval families to training and drop the twin as an eval instrument.
**Recommendation: (a)** — preserves the redistributable eval twin AND the
CC-BY-NC eval-side-only discipline (ASM-1125). [PROPOSED-ASM-1421]

**MD-R2-3 — Rungs.** (a) R1 + R2, sign-only two-rung language. (b) R1 only
(cheapest). **Recommendation: (a)** — the estate's two-rung sign discipline for
any efficiency-flavoured claim; the marginal R2 cost is a few LoRA runs.
[PROPOSED-ASM-1433]

**MD-R2-4 — Proof-augmented arm B3.** (a) Include (rationale distillation,
descriptive only). (b) Drop. **Recommendation: (a) if marginal cost ≤ $3** —
it is the one variant with a distinct mechanistic story (proof text as
scaffold), and it is descriptive, so it inflates no claim.

**MD-R2-5 — Cost ceiling.** (a) Smallest decisive ($4–8). (b) Full skeleton
with a **$18 ceiling**. (c) Worst case ($25–35). **Recommendation: (b)** —
mirrors the MD-6(c) pattern; gate (c) on the primary readout.
[EXTRAPOLATION: PROPOSED-ASM-1434]

**MD-R2-6 — Decode-mask A6 in RULES-2.** (a) Keep deferred (closed 23-word
vocabulary; tautology unchanged). (b) Run it here with the \|L\| ledger.
**Recommendation: (a)** — DV-5's deferral condition ("an open answer surface")
is not met by this design. [PROPOSED-ASM-1435]

**MD-R2-7 — Degradation-guard scope.** (a) Minimal: stated-cell + E5 refusal
strata only. (b) Add a small pinned external anchor set (non-kinship) for a
catastrophic-forgetting probe. **Recommendation: (b) if an existing pinned
anchor can be reused at ~zero authoring cost; else (a)** — the Herron caveat is
about downstream degradation, and a purely in-domain guard under-detects it.
[PROPOSED-ASM-1430]

**MD-R2-8 — Knull claim cap carry-over.** (a) Carry ASM-1138's cap verbatim:
any positive RULES-2 result is "engine-materialised entailments are

ablation runs (a knull-sourced-closure fine-tune is the natural c5 analog

**Recommendation: (a)** — the knull fork (issue #6) is still open; the cap is
free. [PROPOSED-ASM-1438]

## 5. What RULES-2 adds to the two theses

**Correctness:** nothing new is claimed about the engine — its soundness
evidence stays with the RULES-1 certificate. RULES-2's correctness surface is
the trained model's, and it is scored on the same third-party gold with the
same fail-closed refusal stratum trained in, so a model that internalises the
inference but loses the refusal discipline is caught by s4′, not celebrated.

**Efficiency:** this is the experiment's centre of gravity. RULES-1 prices the
engine at inference (µs CPU + k = 4 resamples); A4 prices default-world lookup;
RULES-2 completes the ledger with the third column — amortised training cost
for an engine-free k = 1 inference path — and measures whether that path even
exists (the primary). Whichever way it falls, the maintainer's
train-time/prompt-time split acquires its full empirical price table.
